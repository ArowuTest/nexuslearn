Question Variants

en-y6-grammar-precision-q-passive-focus

meaning-choice review difficulty 6

A report needs to focus on the damaged bridge, not on who inspected it. Which sentence best matches that purpose?

The passive clause foregrounds the damaged bridge and leaves out the inspectors because the report's focus is the bridge.

en-y6-grammar-precision-q-modality

grammar-repair review difficulty 5

The evidence is incomplete, so the writer wants to express a cautious possibility. Choose the best edit: 'The footprint ___ belong to a fox.'

'Might' expresses a cautious possibility. 'Must' and 'will' would make the claim too certain.

en-y6-grammar-precision-q-semicolon

clause-map review difficulty 6

Which sentence uses a semicolon correctly?

'The warning light flashed' and 'the crew checked the control panel' are both complete clauses, so the semicolon is grammatically appropriate.

en-y6-grammar-precision-q-hyphen-ambiguity

grammar-repair review difficulty 7

Which edit clearly means that the school owns a small-animal hospital rather than a small hospital for animals?

The hyphen joins 'small' and 'animal' as a compound modifier, making it clear that the hospital treats small animals.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use a calm editing desk with stable sentence cards and no flashing error marks.

reduced_motion

Replace clause reordering animations with static before-and-after maps and focus outlines.

audio_first

Read sentence alternatives aloud, with optional spoken names for punctuation and clause boundaries.

reading_support

Use line focus, generous spacing, chunked clauses and a persistent glossary of grammatical terms.

attention_support

Ask one decision at a time: intended meaning, grammatical structure, punctuation, then reader effect.

processing_support

Allow repeated comparison, hide irrelevant choices and provide role labels without completing the edit.

confidence_support

Award evidence for a well-explained choice and frame errors as repairable mismatches between grammar and purpose.

eal_support

Preteach subject, object, active, passive, modal and clause through paired examples; allow oral rehearsal before terminology is required.
