Teaching Journey

warm_up

state-property-sort

Sort these properties into solid, liquid or gas.

Reactivate observable properties before particle explanation.

Audio: First sort what we can observe, then we will explain it with particles.

Visual: Property cards move into three labelled chambers.

concept_launch

particle-zoom-lens

Zoom in to see particles in each state.

Introduce particle models as explanations, not literal photographs.

Audio: A model helps us explain what is happening, even when particles are too small to see.

Visual: A zoom lens transitions from material sample to particle model.

worked_example

three-state-particle-compare

Compare how particles are arranged and moving in each state.

Model arrangement and movement together.

Audio: Solid particles vibrate in fixed positions. Liquid particles move past each other. Gas particles are far apart and move freely.

Visual: Three particle chambers animate with different spacing and movement.

guided_practice

energy-slider-melt

Raise the energy and watch the solid melt.

Connect heating to particle movement and arrangement changes.

Audio: When particles gain energy, they move more and may change arrangement.

Visual: An energy slider changes particle movement from solid to liquid.

checkpoint

particle-count-lock

When a solid melts, what happens to the particles?

Detect expansion or destruction misconceptions.

Audio: The particles do not disappear. Their arrangement and movement change.

Visual: Particle count stays the same while arrangement changes.

teach_back

companion-vibration-repair

Teach Lyra why solid particles still move.

Repair the no-motion-in-solids misconception through explanation.

Audio: I thought solid particles were frozen still. Can you show me the vibration?

Visual: The companion pauses a solid model and asks for evidence.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use calm lab colours and avoid rapid particle movement by default.

reduced_motion

Provide static snapshots at low, medium and high energy with optional slow playback.

audio_first

Narrate particle model observations and allow replay of vocabulary cards.

reading_support

Use diagrams, icons and short explanation chunks before dense text.

attention_support

Keep simulation controls limited to one slider or sort action per step.

processing_support

Allow freeze-frame inspection and side-by-side comparisons.

confidence_support

Use model-testing language so misconceptions become hypotheses to check.

eal_support

Preteach particle, solid, liquid, gas, vibrate, arrangement, energy, melting and evaporating.
