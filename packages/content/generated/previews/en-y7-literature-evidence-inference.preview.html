Teaching Journey

warm_up

mood-word-highlight

Choose one word that makes the scene feel tense.

Reactivate word-level evidence before whole-extract inference.

Visual: A short extract with selectable mood words.

concept_launch

analysis-chain-build

Build a literature chain: quote, inference, effect.

Introduce KS3 analysis as linked reasoning.

Audio: A strong literary answer links evidence to an inference and explains the effect.

Visual: Three linked analysis cards on a study desk.

worked_example

quote-shadow-effect

The corridor 'swallowed the last of the light'. What mood does this create?

Model inference from figurative language.

Audio: Swallowed suggests the darkness feels powerful, so the mood may be threatening.

Visual: The quote casts a shadow over the mood dial.

quote-relevance-thread

Practise relevance, not just quote spotting.

Audio: The best quote directly supports the idea.

Visual: Quote cards connect or fail to connect to an inference card.

checkpoint

analysis-bridge-test

Check explanation of writer effect.

Audio: Look for the answer that explains how the words affect the reader.

Visual: Three analysis bridges compete to link quote and effect.

teach_back

companion-analysis-repair

Repair summary-only answers through teach-back.

Audio: I said what happened. Why is that not enough?
