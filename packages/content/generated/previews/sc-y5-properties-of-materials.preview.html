Question Variants

sc-y5-properties-of-materials-q-window-choice

property-test-lab review difficulty 5

A viewing panel must let light through, resist water and keep its shape. Which evidence best supports choosing clear acrylic over absorbent card?

For this viewing panel, the acrylic is supported by evidence that it is transparent, waterproof and rigid; this does not make it best for every use.

sc-y5-properties-of-materials-q-dissolved-salt

claim-evidence-explain review difficulty 6

Salt is stirred into water until no crystals are visible. Which statement is best supported?

The unchanged mass and later recovery support the explanation that salt particles are dispersed through the solution rather than gone.

sc-y5-properties-of-materials-q-separate-mixture

mixture-separation-planner review difficulty 8

A mixture contains iron filings, sand and salt. Choose a valid route to recover the three solids.

Magnetism separates iron; water dissolves salt but not sand; filtration retains sand; removing water from the filtrate recovers salt.

sc-y5-properties-of-materials-q-change-evidence

change-evidence-sort review difficulty 6

Wax melts and later solidifies. A different sample burns and produces smoke and ash. Which conclusion best fits?

Cooling can recover solid wax after melting, while burning produces smoke and ash with new properties and is not usually reversible.

property-test-and-material-choice

property-test-lab expected 320 planned

Compare materials fairly and justify a choice using multiple properties relevant to a purpose.

Use plausible property data and avoid absolute claims such as strongest or best without a defined test and comparison set.

dissolving-solubility-and-recovery

claim-evidence-explain expected 280 planned

Explain dissolving as formation of a solution and use evidence to show the solute remains and may be recovered.

Keep dissolving distinct from melting; do not imply all dissolved substances are safely recoverable or that evaporation should be attempted unsupervised.

single-and-multistage-separation

mixture-separation-planner secure 340 planned

Select and sequence separation methods from differences in particle size, solubility, magnetism and state.

Verify that every proposed route can work under the stated conditions; filtration must not be shown separating a dissolved solute.

reversible-and-irreversible-evidence

change-evidence-sort expected 300 planned

Classify changes from recovery and new-material evidence rather than appearance alone.

Avoid treating one sign such as colour or temperature change as proof by itself; distinguish practical difficulty from scientific irreversibility at age-appropriate depth.

materials-claim-critique-retrieval

claim-evidence-explain retrieval 260 planned

Maintain property, solution, separation and change reasoning through short claim-evidence critiques.

Keep reading demand below the science demand and include uncertainty when evidence does not justify a causal or universal claim.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use a calm virtual laboratory with no strong sound effects, no simulated fumes, one active test at a time and predictable transitions.

reduced_motion

Replace dissolving, filtering, evaporation and change animations with numbered still frames, particle diagrams and complete result tables.

audio_first

Announce material, test condition, observation, measurement and conclusion separately, with replay at every evidence point.

reading_support

Use short method cards, icons plus words for properties, glossary popovers, sentence frames and a linear alternative to flow diagrams.

attention_support

Display QUESTION-PREDICT-TEST-RECORD-EXPLAIN or MIXTURE-PROPERTY-METHOD-CHECK strips and lock irrelevant controls.

processing_support

Remove timers, preserve data, permit repeated virtual trials, expose all graph values in tables and allow oral, typed or structured responses.

confidence_support

Treat predictions as testable, credit accurate observations before explanations and preserve correct route stages during repair.

eal_support

Preteach transparent, flexible, conductor, insulator, soluble, solution, filter, evaporate, reversible and irreversible with objects, diagrams and sentence frames.
