Teaching Journey

warm_up

object-material-trays

Listen to the word. Is it an object or a material?

Activate the central object-material distinction before naming examples.

Audio: A cup is an object. Plastic can be the material. Listen for which kind of word you hear.

Visual: Two large audio-labelled trays: object and material.

concept_launch

made-from-links

Match each object to what it is made from.

Connect familiar objects to common material names while allowing multiple examples.

Audio: Objects can be made from different materials. We check the particular object in front of us.

Visual: A cup shown in reviewed plastic, metal and glass examples beside material sample cards.

worked_example

property-test-before-after

Watch the safe model test. Is the sample bendy or stiff?

Model one controlled property observation and precise comparative language.

Audio: We change one thing gently and watch what happens. This sample bends, so we describe it as bendy.

Visual: Two equal-sized reviewed samples shown before and after a gentle model bend.

guided_practice

property-station-focus

Choose a safe test card, observe the evidence and add a property label.

Practise describing materials using evidence from look, touch alternatives or simulations.

Audio: Choose one property question. You never need to taste, smell, break or handle a real sample.

Visual: Static or modelled stations for bendy/stiff, smooth/rough, transparent/opaque and absorbent/not absorbent.

checkpoint

property-rule-sort

Sort by the spoken rule, then show one piece of evidence.

Check whether grouping follows the named property rather than colour, use or preference.

Audio: The rule is transparent or not transparent. Look for whether you can see clearly through each sample.

Visual: Two audio-labelled hoops and three low-clutter reviewed material samples.

teach_back

companion-spoon-material-repair

Pip says every spoon is metal. Teach Pip how to check the object and its material.

Repair object-material overgeneralisation and consolidate evidence language.

Audio: I know this is a spoon, but how can I tell what this spoon is made from?

Visual: Reviewed metal, wooden and plastic spoons with separate object and material labels.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use plain sample cards, quiet narration, no scraping or pouring sounds by default and no surprise breakage effects.

reduced_motion

Replace bending, drops and sorting travel with static before-and-after evidence frames and instant placement.

audio_first

Speak every object, material, property, rule and explanation with replay; produced narration is reviewed before child release.

reading_support

Use close-up images, property icons and one- or two-word labels; written text never carries the only evidence.

attention_support

Ask one property question at a time and keep non-target controls hidden.

processing_support

Allow unlimited replay, enlarged samples, side-by-side evidence, auto-placement and an honest cannot-decide option.

confidence_support

Credit accurate observation even when vocabulary needs prompting and avoid red failure states or speed scoring.

eal_support

Preteach object, material, made from, wood, plastic, glass, metal, water, rock, bendy, stiff, smooth, rough, transparent and absorbent with real or pictured examples.
