Question Variants

en-y4-paragraphs-q-rainforest-belongs

paragraph-sort review difficulty 3

Which sentence belongs in a paragraph about rainforest animals?

The frog sentence fits the rainforest animals theme; the other sentences move to different ideas.

en-y4-paragraphs-q-topic-stormy-sea

topic-sentence-choice review difficulty 5

Choose the best topic sentence for a paragraph with details about crashing waves, dark clouds and a rocking boat.

The best topic sentence introduces the whole stormy sea idea, not just one detail.

en-y4-paragraphs-q-break-setting-character

paragraph-break-edit review difficulty 6

Where should the paragraph break go when the focus changes?

A break after the setting sentences helps the reader see the focus change from the cave to Maya.

en-y4-paragraphs-q-rogue-sentence-garden

paragraph-sort review difficulty 4

Find the sentence that does not belong in a paragraph about growing vegetables.

The goalkeeper sentence belongs in a different paragraph because it changes the topic to sport.

en-y4-paragraphs-q-link-because

linking-word-choice review difficulty 4

Choose the best linking word: The path was slippery, ___ we walked slowly.

'Because' shows why the children walked slowly: the path was slippery.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use soft idea-island colours, no flashing card effects and optional plain-paper mode.

reduced_motion

Use instant card grouping and static paragraph boundary lines.

audio_first

Read each sentence aloud and replay grouped paragraphs with short pauses at breaks.

reading_support

Use sentence chunking, dyslexia-friendly spacing and glossary popovers for topic, theme and paragraph.

attention_support

Limit visible cards, reveal one decision at a time and show a clear progress trail.

processing_support

Allow reread, undo, theme highlighting and compare-before-submit.

confidence_support

Treat paragraphing as organising a messy draft, with positive feedback for better grouping.

eal_support

Preteach paragraph, theme, topic sentence, detail, focus and link with icons and oral examples.
