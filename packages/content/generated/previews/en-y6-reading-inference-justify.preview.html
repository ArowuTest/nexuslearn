Teaching Journey

warm_up

text-clue-highlight

Highlight one word that shows the character is worried.

Reactivate evidence finding before full inference.

Audio: Find a clue in the words, not just in your head.

Visual: A short sentence with selectable words and a feeling meter.

concept_launch

inference-chain-build

Build an inference chain: clue, inference, because.

Introduce inference as a linked reasoning process.

Audio: A strong inference links the clue to your answer.

Visual: Three linked cards labelled clue, inference and because.

worked_example

evidence-to-inference-thread

She hid the torn map behind her back. What might this suggest?

Model how an action can suggest a motive or feeling.

Audio: Hiding the map is evidence. It suggests she may feel guilty or worried.

Visual: Text highlight connects to inference options.

guided_practice

evidence-card-snap

Choose the best inference, then highlight the words that prove it.

Practise answer and evidence selection together.

Audio: Pick your inference, then prove it with text evidence.

Visual: A short extract with answer cards and highlightable evidence.

checkpoint

because-bridge-test

Which because sentence best justifies the evidence?

Check whether learners explain the evidence rather than only quote it.

Audio: The best because sentence explains how the clue proves the inference.

Visual: Evidence and inference cards with three because bridges.

teach_back

companion-because-repair

Teach Miko why a quote alone is not a full answer.

Use teach-back to repair quote-without-explanation responses.

Audio: I found a quote. Why do I still need to explain it?

Visual: The companion holds a quote card with a missing because bridge.

Question Variants

en-y6-reading-inference-justify-q-worried-clue

evidence-highlight approved difficulty 5

Highlight the words that best show Maya is worried.

Checking the corridor again and twisting the map suggest Maya is worried or nervous.

en-y6-reading-inference-justify-q-because-bridge

reason-builder approved difficulty 6

Which sentence best justifies the inference that Omar is trying to hide something?

The best justification explains how pushing the notebook out of sight supports the inference.

en-y6-reading-inference-justify-q-short-response

short-response approved difficulty 7

What can you infer about Lena's feelings? Use evidence.

A strong response may infer that Lena is proud but nervous, using the smile and shaking hand as evidence.
