Teaching Journey

warm_up

push-pull-card-sort

Sort these actions into push, pull or both.

Reactivate force as interaction before arrow modelling.

Visual: Action cards sort into push, pull and both zones.

concept_launch

force-arrow-builder

Place arrows on the object to show the forces acting on it.

Introduce arrow direction, relative size and point of action using a clean model.

Audio: A force arrow starts on the object and points in the direction of the force.

Visual: A box with draggable or switch-selectable arrows for weight, normal contact, push and friction.

worked_example

book-balanced-forces

Why does the book stay still even though forces act on it?

Show balanced vertical forces and repair the no-forces misconception.

Audio: The book has weight downward and a normal contact force upward. The forces are balanced, so the book's motion does not change.

Visual: Equal upward and downward arrows lock into a free-body-style model.

guided_practice

sledge-resultant-slider

Adjust the push and friction arrows. When will the sledge speed up?

Connect unbalanced horizontal forces to changes in motion.

Audio: If the forward force is larger than the backward force, the resultant force is forwards and the sledge speeds up.

Visual: Force arrows resize, then a resultant arrow appears.

checkpoint

steady-speed-force-balance

A cyclist moves at steady speed. What can you infer about the forward and backward forces?

Detect the misconception that motion always requires unbalanced forward force.

Audio: At steady speed in a straight line, the forces are balanced.

Visual: Equal drive and resistive-force arrows appear while the bike keeps moving steadily.

teach_back

companion-steady-motion-repair

Teach Juno why balanced forces can act on a moving object.

Consolidate force-motion reasoning through explanation.

Audio: I thought balanced meant stopped. Can you explain steady motion?

Visual: Companion compares a parked bike and a steady-speed bike with balanced arrows.

Question Variants

sc-y7-forces-q-book-table

force-arrow-model review difficulty 4

Place the two vertical force arrows on a book resting on a table.

A resting book has weight downward and a normal contact force upward. These forces are balanced.

sc-y7-forces-q-steady-cyclist

explain-choice review difficulty 6

A cyclist travels at steady speed in a straight line. Which force explanation is best?

At steady speed in a straight line, the resultant force is zero because the forward and backward forces are balanced.

sc-y7-forces-q-sledge-speed-up

free-body-style-sort review difficulty 5

Which diagram shows a sledge that will speed up forwards?

The sledge speeds up forwards when the forward push is larger than the backward friction force, giving a forward resultant force.

sc-y7-forces-q-magnet-poe

prediction-observation-explanation review difficulty 7

Predict what happens when a magnet is moved closer to a steel paperclip, observe the arrow model, then choose the best explanation.

Moving the magnet closer can increase the magnetic force on the paperclip, producing a resultant force towards the magnet.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use calm backgrounds, thick arrow outlines and no sudden motion bursts when resultants appear.

reduced_motion

Provide static arrow diagrams and step-through resultant reveals instead of moving objects.

audio_first

Read each force arrow as object, force name, direction and relative size.

reading_support

Pair each force term with an icon and use short sentence frames for explanations.

attention_support

Highlight one axis at a time and hide non-target arrows until needed.

processing_support

Allow freeze-frame comparison, larger arrow handles, described diagrams and undo without penalty.

confidence_support

Treat diagram errors as model edits and show what evidence each arrow represents.

eal_support

Preteach push, pull, force, arrow, balanced, unbalanced, contact, non-contact, friction and gravity.
