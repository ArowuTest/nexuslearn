Teaching Journey

warm_up

digits-align-by-place

Round each number, predict the size of the answer, then place every digit in its correct column.

Activate magnitude and place-value knowledge before procedural work.

Audio: Estimate first, then line up thousands, hundreds, tens and ones.

Visual: Two numbers move into a labelled place-value chart while a check gauge shows the estimated range.

concept_launch

counters-to-written-layout

A written method keeps each place-value calculation organised so no value is lost.

Frame column methods as recorded place-value reasoning rather than a memorised digit routine.

Audio: Each column has a value. We calculate from the ones and record every exchange.

Visual: Place-value counters sit above matching written digits in four columns.

worked_example

exchange-and-record

For 2,786 + 1,457, add the ones first. Thirteen ones become 3 ones and 1 exchanged ten. Continue one column at a time.

Model addition with consecutive exchanges and connect every written mark to quantity.

Audio: Ten ones are exchanged for one ten. The total stays the same as the counters move to a new column.

Visual: Counters exchange beside the calculation; the small exchanged digit appears only after the counters regroup.

guided_practice

guided-column-reveal

Build the calculation, choose whether to exchange, and enter one column result at a time.

Fade from concrete counters to an independent written layout while preserving place-value language.

Audio: Say the value of the column, make any exchange, then record the digit.

Visual: A split view pairs draggable counters with keyboard-editable calculation cells.

checkpoint

first-error-freeze

A learner says 4,032 - 1,758 = 3,326. Find the first column where the method stops representing the calculation.

Detect larger-from-smaller digit subtraction and insecure exchange across a zero.

Audio: Do not swap the digits. Rename a larger place-value unit and keep the total equivalent.

Visual: The incorrect calculation can be inspected column by column against place-value counters.

teach_back

inverse-check-loop

Teach your companion how an estimate and the inverse can check 3,641 - 1,286.

Gather explanation evidence and connect calculation accuracy to independent checking.

Audio: Explain what answer size you expect, then show how addition can undo the subtraction.

Visual: An estimate gauge and inverse number sentence appear beside the completed calculation.

Question Variants

ma-y4-written-methods-q-align

place-value-layout review difficulty 3

Place 3,406 and 782 into the grid ready for addition.

Digits are aligned by place value, so 6 and 2 are in the ones column and 4 and 7 are in the hundreds column.

ma-y4-written-methods-q-add-exchanges

column-calculate review difficulty 6

Calculate 2,786 + 1,457. Record every exchange.

The exchanges preserve the total: 13 ones becomes 1 ten and 3 ones, then the tens and hundreds also require an exchange, giving 4,243.

ma-y4-written-methods-q-subtract-zero

column-calculate review difficulty 8

Calculate 4,032 - 1,758 and use addition to check.

Renaming one hundred as ten tens and then one ten as ten ones makes the subtraction possible. The difference is 2,274, and 2,274 + 1,758 = 4,032.

ma-y4-written-methods-q-first-error

error-analysis review difficulty 7

A learner calculated 3,504 - 1,268 = 2,344. Select the first incorrect column and repair it.

The first error is in the ones column. The learner appears to have subtracted 4 from 8 instead of exchanging. The correct answer is 2,236.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use a plain four-column grid, one static highlight at a time, no celebratory particle effects and a persistent mute control.

reduced_motion

Replace moving counters with before/after exchange panels; preserve the same values, narration and focus order.

audio_first

Read the whole calculation, then announce the active column and its value; provide replay buttons beside every worked step.

reading_support

Pair words with symbols, keep instructions to one action per sentence and leave the calculation visible while hints are read.

attention_support

Reveal only the current column, show a six-step progress strip and pause automatically after each exchange until the learner continues.

processing_support

Remove timers, allow unlimited estimate revisions and provide a no-drag stepper interface with undo.

confidence_support

Label incorrect entries as a place-value clue, retain correct columns during repair and praise checking or strategy changes.

eal_support

Preteach place value, column, exchange, estimate, sum, difference and inverse with counters and dual-coded labels.
