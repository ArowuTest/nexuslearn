Teaching Journey

warm_up

whole-size-align

Which two bars show the same whole?

Check fair-comparison understanding before equivalence.

Audio: Equivalent fractions need the same whole.

Visual: Aligned and misaligned fraction bars compare whole sizes.

concept_launch

fraction-bar-subdivide

Split one half into smaller equal parts without changing its value.

Introduce equivalence as repartitioning the same amount.

Audio: The shaded amount stays the same, even when the pieces get smaller.

Visual: A half bar subdivides into two quarters and then four eighths.

worked_example

fraction-scale-lens

One half is equivalent to two quarters.

Connect area model to symbolic numerator and denominator scaling.

Audio: Both the shaded parts and total parts doubled, so the value stayed the same.

Visual: A x2 lens transforms 1/2 into 2/4.

guided_practice

symbol-and-bar-sync

Use the scale lens to build a fraction equivalent to 3/5.

Practise multiplying numerator and denominator by the same factor.

Audio: Whatever factor you use, use it on the top and the bottom.

Visual: A symbolic fraction links to a fraction bar that repartitions.

checkpoint

equivalent-line-align

Do 2/3 and 4/6 land on the same point?

Check equivalence across representations.

Audio: Equivalent fractions land at the same place on a number line.

Visual: Two stacked number lines align 2/3 and 4/6.

teach_back

companion-denominator-repair

Teach Ari why 3/6 is not bigger than 1/2.

Repair denominator-size misconceptions through explanation.

Audio: Six pieces sounds like more. Can you show me why the value is the same?

Visual: The companion compares a half bar with a sixths bar.

Question Variants

ma-y5-number-fractions-equivalence-q-half-quarters

fraction-bar approved difficulty 4

Which fraction is equivalent to 1/2?

1/2 and 2/4 are equivalent because they shade the same amount of the same whole.

ma-y5-number-fractions-equivalence-q-scale-3-5

symbol-build approved difficulty 5

Multiply 3/5 by a scale factor of 2. What equivalent fraction do you get?

3/5 is equivalent to 6/10 because both numerator and denominator are multiplied by 2.

number-line approved difficulty 6

Do 2/3 and 4/6 land on the same point on the number line?

2/3 and 4/6 are equivalent, so they land at the same point.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use clean lab colours and avoid high-speed subdivision animations.

Use step-by-step static fraction panels and line alignment.

audio_first

Narrate fraction names, equivalent meaning and scale steps.

reading_support

Pair symbolic fractions with visual bars and spoken explanations.

attention_support

Keep one representation active at a time before syncing all three.

processing_support

Allow learners to slow subdivision and inspect numerator-denominator changes.

confidence_support
