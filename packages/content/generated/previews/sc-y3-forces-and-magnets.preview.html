Teaching Journey

warm_up

force-effect-compare

For each picture, choose push or pull and name what changed: start, stop, speed, direction or shape.

Connect force language to observable effects before comparing specific interactions.

Audio: Look for the effect. The object may start, stop, speed up, slow down, turn or change shape.

Visual: Paired still frames show before and after with a labelled effect menu.

concept_launch

contact-gap-sort

Sort interactions into NEEDS TOUCH and CAN ACT ACROSS A GAP.

Distinguish contact forces from the observed non-contact action of magnets.

Audio: A hand must touch the cart, but a magnet can move a magnetic object across a small gap.

Visual: A contact icon and a measured-gap icon label each interaction.

worked_example

surface-distance-fair-test

The same toy car is released from the same ramp onto smooth card and rough fabric. Compare the distances.

Model a fair surface test and avoid explaining results from surface labels alone.

Audio: Keep the car and release point the same. Change the surface, measure the distance and compare repeated trials.

Visual: Two tracks share car and ramp-height labels, with ruler and repeat-results table.

guided_practice

magnetic-material-test

Test one object at a time, record whether it is attracted, then group the materials using your results.

Build material classification from observations rather than appearance or the word metal.

Audio: Predict, test across a small gap, record the result, and use the evidence to group the objects.

Visual: A safe virtual magnet, mixed everyday objects, gap marker and results table.

checkpoint

pole-orientation-compare

Predict what happens when north faces north, then turn one magnet so north faces south.

Check pole-based attraction and repulsion instead of memorising that magnets always pull.

Audio: Matching poles repel. Different poles attract.

Visual: N and S labels remain visible while direction arrows and the gap change.

teach_back

prediction-evidence-reconcile

Teach the companion why 'shiny' or 'metal' is not enough evidence that an object will be attracted.

Secure cautious classification and the need for a test result.

Audio: Name the material if known, describe the magnet test and use the result in your conclusion.

Visual: Prediction and observation cards stay separate until the conclusion is spoken.

Question Variants

sc-y3-forces-and-magnets-q-surface-test

surface-test review difficulty 5

The same car is released from the same point. Which conclusion fits the results?

The repeated measurements are close together and show a greater distance on smooth card in this controlled setup.

sc-y3-forces-and-magnets-q-contact-gap

contact-force-sort review difficulty 3

Which interaction can make an object move across a small gap without touching it?

The magnet can attract the steel paperclip before touching it; the other examples are contact forces.

sc-y3-forces-and-magnets-q-materials

magnetic-material-test review difficulty 6

A steel washer is attracted. An aluminium foil ball and a copper coin are not. Which conclusion matches the evidence?

The steel object was attracted, while the aluminium and copper objects were not, so the evidence does not support an all-metals rule.

sc-y3-forces-and-magnets-q-poles

pole-predictor review difficulty 5

The north pole of one bar magnet faces the north pole of another. What will happen?

Two north poles are matching poles, so the magnets repel and move away if free to move.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use a quiet virtual lab, muted surfaces, no impact sounds, no sudden magnet snaps and one active test at a time.

reduced_motion

Replace rolling, attracting and repelling animation with numbered still frames, distance values and towards-or-away labels.

audio_first

Announce setup, changed variable, observation and conclusion in order; replay pole names and table values on request.

reading_support

Use short test cards, icons for change/keep same/measure, glossary popovers and data tables alongside every visual result.

attention_support

Use a fixed PREDICT-TEST-RECORD-EXPLAIN routine and lock controls unrelated to the current comparison.

processing_support

Remove timers, allow repeated trials, preserve results and provide side-by-side static comparisons before explanation.

confidence_support

Treat predictions as testable ideas, reward updating a claim after evidence and keep correct records during repair.

eal_support

Preteach push, pull, touch, gap, surface, attract, repel, pole and material with gestures, objects and sentence frames.
