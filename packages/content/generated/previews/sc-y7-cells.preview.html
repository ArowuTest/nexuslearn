Teaching Journey

warm_up

living-levels-zoom

Order organism, organ, tissue and cell from largest level to smallest.

Locate cells within biological organisation before focusing on internal cell structures.

Audio: Organisms contain organ systems, organs contain tissues, and tissues are made from cells.

Visual: Nested biological levels zoom from organism to cell.

concept_launch

micrograph-model-link

Compare a microscope image with a simplified cell model. What can each representation show?

Establish that observations and diagrams are related but serve different purposes.

Audio: A microscope image provides observational evidence. A model simplifies features so we can identify and explain them.

Visual: A microscope field and a labelled model appear side by side with evidence links.

worked_example

animal-cell-feature-trace

Trace the boundary, nucleus and cytoplasm, then match each structure to its function.

Model accurate identification from position and visible evidence, followed by structure-function reasoning.

Audio: The membrane forms the cell boundary, the cytoplasm fills much of the cell, and the nucleus contains genetic material and helps control activities.

Visual: An animal-cell micrograph is progressively outlined and linked to a clean model.

guided_practice

cell-compare-zones

Build a comparison showing shared structures and structures usually shown only in the plant-cell model.

Practise precise comparison without relying on stereotyped overall shapes.

Audio: Both plant and animal cells have a membrane, cytoplasm and genetic material. Plant-cell models also show a cellulose cell wall, chloroplasts and a permanent vacuole.

Visual: Two cell panels feed structure cards into shared and distinct comparison zones.

checkpoint

wall-membrane-boundary-test

Which statement correctly distinguishes the cell wall from the cell membrane?

Detect whether learners separate support from selective exchange.

Audio: The cellulose cell wall supports the plant cell. The cell membrane controls movement of substances into and out of the cell.

Visual: An outer support layer and inner selective boundary respond differently to test arrows.

teach_back

companion-model-limit-repair

Teach Nova why a coloured cell diagram is useful but is not a literal photograph of the cell.

Consolidate model literacy and prevent colour, scale and shape conventions from becoming false biological claims.

Audio: Which features come from observation, and which features have been simplified to help us reason?

Visual: The companion toggles labels, false colour and scale warnings over a cell diagram.

Question Variants

sc-y7-cells-q-label-animal

cell-label review difficulty 4

Label the cell membrane, cytoplasm and nucleus on the animal-cell model.

The cell membrane forms the cell boundary, the cytoplasm occupies much of the interior and the nucleus is a distinct internal structure containing genetic material.

sc-y7-cells-q-compare

compare-model review difficulty 5

Sort the structures into shared by the two models, plant-cell model only, or neither.

Both simplified models contain a membrane, cytoplasm and nucleus. The plant-cell model additionally shows a cellulose cell wall, chloroplasts and a permanent vacuole; bone is a tissue, not a cell structure.

sc-y7-cells-q-wall-membrane

function-choice review difficulty 6

Which statement correctly distinguishes the cell wall from the cell membrane in a plant cell?

The cellulose cell wall provides support, whereas the cell membrane controls the movement of substances into and out of the cell.

sc-y7-cells-q-model-limit

evidence-explanation review difficulty 7

A diagram shows the nucleus bright purple and much larger than in a microscope image. Which conclusion is best?

Scientific diagrams often use false colour, clear boundaries and altered scale to communicate structures. Those conventions should not be mistaken for direct evidence of natural colour or relative size.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use calm microscope controls, steady focus changes and no flickering, flashing or rapid zoom effects.

reduced_motion

Provide static focus stages, side-by-side images and outlined snapshots instead of continuous zoom or scanning.

audio_first

Describe each cell image systematically from boundary to interior and provide replayable structure-function cards.

reading_support

Use short labels, symbol-supported definitions, line-by-line explanations and a persistent glossary beside the image.

attention_support

Highlight one target structure at a time and limit the visible label bank to the current comparison or function task.

processing_support

Allow freeze-frame inspection, enlarged print views, pre-labelled reference models and extra time before explanation.

confidence_support

Credit accurate observation separately from naming and function, then give one targeted repair prompt.

eal_support

Preteach cell, structure, function, boundary, membrane, wall, cytoplasm, nucleus, mitochondria and chloroplast with paired image and action language.
