Teaching Journey

warm_up

sound-seeds-light

Tap the sounds you already know.

Activate known grapheme-phoneme links before blending.

Audio: Let's wake up the sounds first.

Visual: Three calm sound seeds light up one at a time.

concept_launch

blend-river-sweep

The sound seeds need to slide together to grow the word.

Introduce blending as a continuous sweep, not separate sounds.

Audio: Listen: c...a...t. Now sweep them together: cat.

Visual: Seeds move along a small river and join into a word sprout.

worked_example

phoneme-pulse-join

Listen to c-a-t. Sweep the sounds: cat.

Model oral blending with a simple CVC word.

Audio: c, a, t. Sweep: cat.

Visual: Each grapheme pulses as the audio plays, then joins into cat.

guided_practice

stardust-sweep

Tap each sound, then slide your finger to blend.

Move from isolated sounds to continuous blending.

Audio: Tap, tap, tap. Now sweep.

Visual: Large touch targets with a sweep trail.

checkpoint

middle-sound-glow

Which word did you hear: cat or cot?

Check vowel attention rather than picture guessing.

Audio: Listen for the middle sound.

Visual: Two picture cards with sound buttons.

teach_back

companion-repeat-blend

Show Nixi how to sweep c-a-t into cat.

Use teach-back to confirm the blending process.

Audio: Can you help me blend the sounds?

Visual: The companion repeats the child's selected sound path.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use soft tile glow, no burst effects and quiet audio feedback.

reduced_motion

Replace sweeping motion with step-by-step tile highlighting.

audio_first

Every prompt is playable as audio; text remains large and minimal.

reading_support

Use picture support only after sound check, with large grapheme tiles.

attention_support

Keep missions to three words with a predictable tap-sweep-choose routine.

processing_support

Allow unlimited replay and no timers.

confidence_support

Praise careful listening and repair attempts before correctness.

eal_support

Use clear picture anchors after phoneme work and repeat target vocabulary aloud.
