listening-cards-soft-focus

Listen to the two words. What sound do they share?

Attend to the target sound before seeing spelling choices.

concept_launch

grapheme-evidence-reveal

Reveal the words and circle the letters that spell the shared sound.

Connect a heard phoneme to an explicitly taught grapheme pattern.

Audio: Now look closely. Which letters are doing the sound-writing job?

Visual: Word cards reveal and the target graphemes receive identical underlines.

guided_practice

word-card-pattern-sort

The same sound can be written in more than one way. Sort each word by its grapheme.

Prevent overgeneralisation by comparing teacher-approved contrasting word sets.

Audio: Listen, then check the letters. We learn which spelling belongs in each word.

Visual: Pattern columns receive whole-word cards, not isolated rule guesses.

worked_example

y-to-i-suffix-step

Watch happy change to happier. What changed before we added er?

Model a Year 2 suffix transformation while preserving the base word's meaning.

Audio: Happy ends consonant y. Change y to i, then add er: happier.

Visual: The final y tile turns to i before the suffix tile joins.

checkpoint

sound-box-word-check

Listen to the word, choose its pattern, build it, then compare every grapheme.

Check an SSP-aligned listen-segment-build-check routine without handwriting or speech-scoring barriers.

Audio: Use the produced recording. Build left to right and check each part.

Visual: Sound boxes, taught grapheme tiles and a covered answer card.

teach_back

specialist-audio-gate

Teach Pip why we cannot use browser speech or guess any spelling that sounds the same.

Confirm that pronunciation needs specialist recordings and spelling requires word-specific pattern knowledge.

Audio: How do I listen safely and then check the spelling evidence?

Visual: Pip chooses between a gated human recording and a disabled browser-TTS button, then checks grapheme tiles.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use plain word cards, no background music under speech, muted confirmation and no bouncing letters.

reduced_motion

Show static listen-sort-check and base-change-suffix panels.

audio_first

Use produced whole-word recordings only after specialist listening approval; pure phoneme clips are separately gated and browser TTS is never used.

reading_support

Show one word per row, generous spacing, grapheme underlines and taught tiles only.

attention_support

Use one target contrast per screen and a consistent listen-look-build-check routine.

processing_support

Allow unlimited replay of approved assets, slow adult-led modelling, pause between transformations and undo.

confidence_support

Accept tile placement, pointing or adult observation; do not require microphone input or public spelling speed.

eal_support

Confirm word meaning with a picture first, then remove picture cues; never mark accent differences as spelling failure.
