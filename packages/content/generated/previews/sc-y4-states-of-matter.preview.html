Teaching Journey

warm_up

property-test-sequence

Observe each sample: does it keep its own shape, flow to take the container's shape, or spread to fill the available space?

Activate property-based classification instead of relying on familiar object names.

Audio: Classify what the material does, not whether it feels hard or can be poured.

Visual: Safe virtual samples are tilted, compressed or transferred between transparent containers.

concept_launch

state-property-compare

Solids keep their own shape, liquids flow and keep a fixed volume, and gases spread through the available space.

Build a comparative model from observable behaviour while allowing difficult cases such as powders.

Audio: A pile of grains can pour, but each grain remains a small solid with its own shape.

Visual: A comparison table records shape, flow and container behaviour for all three states.

worked_example

water-state-cycle

Ice, liquid water and water vapour are the same material in different states. Heating or cooling changes the state.

Model reversible state change without suggesting that material is created, destroyed or changed into a different substance.

Audio: The water changes state, but it is still water. Follow the arrows for melting, freezing, evaporation and condensation.

Visual: A closed water journey links solid, liquid and gas with labelled heating and cooling arrows and an unchanged amount marker.

guided_practice

temperature-state-linked

Change the temperature, observe the state, record degrees Celsius and name the change.

Connect measured temperature, observation and state-change vocabulary.

Audio: Move the temperature in safe steps. Record what you observe before naming the change.

Visual: A simulation shows a sample, thermometer, observation table and heating/cooling controls.

checkpoint

evaporation-evidence-trace

A puddle becomes smaller on a warm day. Has the water vanished, soaked through the tray, or changed into a gas?

Detect disappearance and evaporation/boiling misconceptions using evidence.

Audio: The liquid water changes state at its surface and mixes with the air as water vapour.

Visual: A water-level graph and covered-container comparison show material transfer rather than disappearance.

teach_back

condensation-source-reveal

Teach your companion why droplets form on the outside of a cold can even when the can does not leak.

Use explanation evidence to connect cooling, water vapour and condensation.

Audio: Where was the water before the droplets appeared, and what change of state happened?

Visual: A sealed cold can sits on a dry card while droplets form only on the cool outer surface.

Question Variants

sc-y4-states-of-matter-q-sand

property-sort review difficulty 5

Sand can be poured. Which observation best shows that sand is made of solids?

Each grain of sand is a small solid that keeps its own shape, even though many grains can flow past one another when poured.

sc-y4-states-of-matter-q-ice-heating

state-change-model review difficulty 4

An ice cube is heated until it becomes liquid water. Name the change and decide whether it is still the same material.

Heating causes the solid ice to melt into liquid water. It is the same material in a different state.

sc-y4-states-of-matter-q-evaporation

evidence-explain review difficulty 6

Two equal puddles are left in trays. The warm tray's puddle becomes smaller first. Which explanation fits the evidence?

The warmer puddle evaporates faster: more liquid water changes into water vapour at the surface, even though it does not boil.

sc-y4-states-of-matter-q-condensation

evidence-explain review difficulty 7

Droplets form outside a sealed cold can standing on dry card. Explain where the droplets come from.

Water vapour in the air is cooled by the can and changes from gas to liquid. This change is condensation.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use quiet laboratory scenes, no bubbling audio by default, no sudden vapour clouds and one observation panel at a time.

reduced_motion

Replace pouring, spreading and state-change motion with labelled before/after stills and a user-controlled next-step button.

audio_first

Announce sample, test, temperature and observation in that order; provide replay without adding facts not visible in the evidence.

reading_support

Use icons beside solid, liquid, gas, heating and cooling; chunk explanations; and provide a glossary without revealing answers.

attention_support

Lock unused lab controls, show OBSERVE-CLASSIFY-EXPLAIN progress and pause the simulation automatically at each evidence point.

processing_support

Remove timers, allow temperature changes in single steps, preserve the observation table and provide undo/reset without penalty.

confidence_support

Treat surprising samples as scientist challenges, keep correct observations during reclassification and reward evidence updates.

eal_support

Preteach state, solid, liquid, gas, melt, freeze, evaporate, condense, heat, cool and degrees Celsius with paired diagrams and sentence frames.
