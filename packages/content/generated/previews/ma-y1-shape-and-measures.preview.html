Teaching Journey

warm_up

feature-outline-calm

Look or feel carefully. Tap one feature you notice.

Activate observation and comparison language without requiring shape naming.

Audio: Notice the edge, corner, surface or end. There is no need to answer quickly.

Visual: One large object or shape with optional outline, corner and surface hotspots.

concept_launch

flat-solid-sort

Sort the flat shapes and solid shapes.

Establish the distinction between 2-D flat shapes and 3-D solid shapes.

Audio: Flat shapes can be drawn on a card. Solid shapes can be held and turned.

Visual: A flat-shape mat and solid-shape tray with audio-labelled examples.

worked_example

ribbon-align-cube-measure

Which ribbon is longer? Then measure it with equal cubes.

Model direct comparison, aligned starts and equal non-standard units.

Audio: First line up the ends. Then place equal cubes with no gaps or overlaps.

Visual: Two ribbons share a start line; cubes fill the longer ribbon end to end.

guided_practice

measure-station-focus

Choose a station: length, mass or capacity. Compare, then tell how you know.

Practise practical comparison using appropriate models and spoken reasoning.

Audio: Use the tool that matches the question. You may tap, move, point or tell an adult.

Visual: A ruler-free cube strip, balance scale and cup-fill board shown one at a time.

checkpoint

spoken-clue-choice

Listen, then choose the picture, coin or movement that matches.

Check transfer to event order, coin value and positional language without heavy reading.

Audio: Hear the whole prompt. Replay it if you need to. Then choose.

Visual: One of three low-clutter boards: routine sequence, UK coin choices or object-position scene.

teach_back

companion-unit-gap-repair

Pip left gaps between the cubes. Teach Pip how to measure fairly.

Consolidate equal-unit iteration and explanation through a repair task.

Audio: I counted six cubes, but I left gaps. Can you fix my measuring and explain why?

Visual: A deliberately gapped cube line beside an empty aligned measuring lane.

shape-feature-and-family-sorts

shape-sort intro 320 planned

Recognise common 2-D and 3-D shapes across varied colours, sizes and orientations.

Include non-prototypical triangles and rotated rectangles; reject distorted drawings and avoid defining a shape by colour.

position-move developing 260 planned

Follow and describe simple position, direction, whole turns, half turns and quarter turns.

State the reference object clearly; offer static before-and-after frames and never require rapid animation tracking.

fair-comparison-and-unit-measure

measure-compare expected 360 planned

Compare and begin to measure length, height, mass and capacity using fair practical methods.

Units must be equal with no hidden gaps; mass and capacity claims must be demonstrated rather than inferred from appearance.

Sequence familiar events, compare simple durations and recognise UK coin values.

Use current UK denominations, avoid price knowledge, and separate event order or duration from formal clock reading.

Retain shape features, fair comparison and practical measure language across spaced review.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use plain mats, quiet confirmation, no background music beneath speech and one comparison at a time.

reduced_motion

Replace turns, pours, balance movement and object travel with static before-and-after frames and instant state changes.

audio_first

Speak every prompt, shape name, positional word, measure comparison and coin denomination with replay and visible playback state.

reading_support

Use objects, shape outlines, symbols and short labels; written instructions never carry the only clue.

attention_support

Use a predictable notice-compare-check-say routine and hide tools not needed for the current measure.

processing_support

Allow unlimited replay, auto-alignment, auto-placement, extra wait time and stepwise reveals.

confidence_support

Preserve correct observations, distinguish motor placement from mathematical judgement and use neutral try-another-way repair.

eal_support

Preteach flat, solid, side, corner, surface, longer, shorter, heavier, lighter, full, empty, before, after and value with objects and gestures.
