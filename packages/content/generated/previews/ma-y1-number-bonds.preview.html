Teaching Journey

warm_up

seed-group-calm-reveal

Look, then tap how many seeds there are.

Activate subitising and careful one-to-one counting before splitting sets.

Audio: See the whole group first. You can count if you need to.

Visual: Up to ten large seeds arranged in familiar dice and ten-frame patterns.

concept_launch

basket-to-pots-split

Move the seeds into two pots. The whole stays the same.

Establish conservation of the whole while the parts change.

Audio: We can split the seeds in different ways. No seeds appear or disappear.

Visual: One basket of seven seeds above two empty pots.

worked_example

roots-join-flower-five

Two seeds and three seeds make five seeds.

Model a number bond with objects, part-whole language and a matching equation.

Audio: The parts are two and three. The whole is five. Two and three make five.

Visual: Five counters split 2 and 3, with a quiet 2 plus 3 equals 5 strip revealed last.

guided_practice

ten-frame-spaces-fill

Fill the frame. How many more make ten?

Build fluent complements to ten using an anchored visual structure.

Audio: Look at the empty spaces. They show the missing part.

Visual: A large ten-frame with counters and tappable empty spaces.

checkpoint

bond-card-turn-and-cover

Seven and three make ten. What else do we know?

Check commutativity and connection to related subtraction without increasing reading load.

Audio: The parts can swap places. If ten loses seven, three is left.

Visual: A reversible part-whole mat and one covered part.

teach_back

companion-uncover-missing-pot

Show Pip how to find the missing part of eight.

Use explanation and reconstruction to confirm part-whole reasoning.

Audio: I can see five, but the whole is eight. How can I find the hidden part?

Visual: Five visible counters, a whole label of eight and a covered second pot.

Question Variants

ma-y1-number-bonds-q-split-five

part-whole-build review difficulty 2

There are five seeds. Put two in the blue pot. How many go in the other pot?

Two and three are the two parts. Together they make the whole, five.

ma-y1-number-bonds-q-make-ten

ten-frame-split review difficulty 3

Six spaces are full. How many more counters make ten?

Six counters and four more counters fill the ten-frame, so 6 and 4 make 10.

ma-y1-number-bonds-q-related-fact

tap-choice review difficulty 5

Eight and two make ten. Tap the take-away fact that matches.

The whole is 10. Taking away the part 8 leaves the other part, 2.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use a plain background, muted counter colours, optional sound effects and no confetti or flashing.

reduced_motion

Replace flying counters with instant state changes and a static before-and-after part-whole model.

audio_first

Speak every instruction, number and equation with replay; audio describes the model without requiring written decoding.

reading_support

Use numerals, counters, frames and speaker icons; written prompts stay to one short sentence.

attention_support

Present one whole and one action at a time, hide unused controls and use predictable build-check-say routines.

processing_support

Offer unlimited thinking time, repeatable count audio, a pause after each counter move and worked-example replay.

confidence_support

Treat incomplete bonds as models to rebuild, preserve the child's placed counters and avoid red failure states.

eal_support

Preteach whole, part, altogether, missing, more and left with gesture and matched picture models.
