Teaching Journey

warm_up

weather-clue-focus

Listen and look. Choose one weather word that matches the evidence.

Practise describing current weather without inferring a season.

Audio: Notice the sky, rain and wind clues. We are describing today, not guessing the whole season.

Visual: One calm window scene with optional cloud, rain and wind indicators.

concept_launch

season-wheel-calm-turn

Meet the four seasons around the year wheel.

Introduce seasons as repeating parts of the year rather than four weather labels.

Audio: Spring, summer, autumn and winter come in a repeating order. Many things can change as the year goes round.

Visual: Four labelled, dated local-observation panels arranged in a circle.

worked_example

dated-tree-compare

What changed on this tree between the two visits?

Model comparison of the same place or organism at different times.

Audio: In the first picture the branches have many leaves. Later, fewer leaves remain. We compare what we can see.

Visual: The same deciduous tree in two clearly dated photographs or reviewed illustrations.

guided_practice

season-evidence-trays

Sort each observation into weather, plant change or daylight.

Separate kinds of evidence and avoid deciding from one stereotyped clue.

Audio: Ask: is this evidence about today's weather, a plant changing, or how long it stays light?

Visual: Three audio-labelled evidence trays with one observation card at a time.

checkpoint

daylight-strip-compare

Which day has more daylight? Does that tell us today's weather?

Check daylight comparison and distinguish it from temperature or cloud.

Audio: Compare the light parts of the day strips. Daylight length does not tell us whether today is sunny or rainy.

Visual: Two equal day strips with longer and shorter daylight sections plus separate weather cards.

teach_back

companion-season-clue-repair

Pip says, 'It cannot be winter because there is no snow.' Teach Pip what evidence to check.

Repair fixed-weather season stereotypes through multi-clue reasoning.

Audio: I looked for snow only. What other observations could help me?

Visual: A mild winter observation card, date marker, bare deciduous tree and short-daylight strip.

Question Variants

sc-y1-seasonal-changes-q-rainy-summer

weather-sort review difficulty 4

It is raining on a July observation day. Which statement is scientifically careful?

Rain can happen in any season, so one rainy day does not rule out summer.

sc-y1-seasonal-changes-q-tree-change

season-observe review difficulty 3

What changed on the same tree between the two dated pictures?

The same tree has fewer leaves and the visible leaf colour has changed.

sc-y1-seasonal-changes-q-evergreen

picture-sequence review difficulty 4

This evergreen tree still has green needles in winter. Is that possible?

Evergreen trees can keep green leaves or needles in winter.

sc-y1-seasonal-changes-q-daylight

daylight-compare review difficulty 5

Which strip shows more daylight?

Strip B has a longer light section, so it represents more daylight.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use still scenes, no background weather audio by default, gentle narration and no simulated lightning, thunder or gust effects.

reduced_motion

Replace season-wheel turns, moving clouds and daylight transitions with static dated panels and outlined differences.

audio_first

Use produced, human-listened narration for every observation, date cue, weather word and comparison; replay never reduces evidence.

reading_support

Use clear scenes, weather symbols and spoken labels; dates are adult-readable but not required for child decoding.

attention_support

Show one observation card at a time and use the repeated notice-record-compare routine.

processing_support

Allow long inspection time, zoomed stills, side-by-side comparison, repeated audio and adult-described images.

confidence_support

Credit accurate weather description even when season reasoning needs repair and avoid presenting uncertain real-world weather as a trick.

eal_support

Preteach season names, weather, sunny, cloudy, rainy, windy, leaf, change, daylight, longer and shorter using pictures and gestures.
