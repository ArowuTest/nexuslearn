Teaching Journey

warm_up

equal-group-copy

If one quest badge needs 3 gems, how many gems do 4 badges need?

Activate multiplicative equal-group thinking before ratio tables.

Audio: Scaling keeps the same groups.

Visual: One badge has three gems; four badges copy the group four times.

concept_launch

ratio-rail-lock

The academy recipe only works if both quantities scale together.

Introduce ratio as a relationship that must be preserved.

Audio: If one side is multiplied, the matching side must be multiplied too.

Visual: A ratio table locks two quantities together with a scale rail.

worked_example

ratio-table-scale

2 blue crystals match 5 red crystals. For 6 blue crystals, multiply both parts by 3.

Model finding a scale factor and applying it to both parts.

Audio: Two becomes six by multiplying by three, so five also multiplies by three.

Visual: A table row scales from 2:5 to 6:15.

guided_practice

scale-token-duplicate

Drag the scale factor onto both matching quantities.

Make the same multiplicative operation visible on both sides.

Audio: The same scale factor travels to both quantities.

Visual: A multiplier token snaps onto both columns.

checkpoint

additive-trap-split

Did this learner add 4 or multiply by 3?

Detect additive reasoning in a proportional situation.

Audio: Check whether the relationship stayed the same.

Visual: Two solution paths compare adding and multiplying.

teach_back

coach-one-side-error

Teach the coach why both quantities must scale together.

Use explanation to confirm multiplicative relationship.

Audio: Can you show why the same multiplier belongs on both sides?

Visual: The coach scales one column only and waits for correction.
