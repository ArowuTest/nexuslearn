Teaching Journey

warm_up

mouth-to-ear-gentle-cue

Say the word, then tap your ear to listen closely.

Establish attention to a spoken word without print or picture guessing.

Audio: Say the whole word once. Now get ready to listen inside it.

Visual: A familiar object picture, a mouth icon and an ear icon with no graphemes shown.

concept_launch

whole-word-to-sound-carriages

Stretch the word and give each sound its own carriage.

Introduce segmenting as ordered phonemes within one whole spoken word.

Audio: The word is sun. Stretch it slowly and listen for each sound.

Visual: A picture of a sun above three empty train carriages used as sound boxes.

worked_example

sound-counter-left-to-right

Watch one counter move for each sound in sat.

Model oral segmenting before grapheme selection, with explicit attention to the middle vowel.

Audio: Say sat. Stretch it. I hear three sounds. One counter goes in each box.

Visual: Three sound boxes fill left to right; grapheme tiles are revealed only after oral counting.

guided_practice

counter-to-grapheme-replace

Push a counter for each sound, then choose the matching letters.

Link oral phoneme order to grapheme selection without handwriting demand.

Audio: Listen first. Push one counter per sound. Then build the word in the same order.

Visual: Three large sound boxes above a small set of known grapheme tiles.

checkpoint

middle-box-calm-focus

Which counter is the middle sound in map?

Check that all phonemes, especially the medial vowel, are represented.

Audio: Stretch the word. Touch each counter. Which sound comes between the first and last?

Visual: Three counters with the centre position outlined, plus replay and adult-recorded word audio.

teach_back

companion-missing-middle-repair

Teach Nixi how to hear every sound in dog.

Confirm a repeatable say-stretch-push-check process through explanation.

Audio: I heard the first and last sounds. How do I find the sound in the middle?

Visual: Nixi shows two counters with a visible empty middle sound box.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use a plain sound-box tray, quiet confirmation tones, no bouncing letters and no background music beneath speech.

reduced_motion

Replace moving counters and trains with static box highlighting in left-to-right order.

audio_first

Use produced, human-listened whole-word audio. Pure phoneme audio is unavailable until SSP-specialist approval; browser TTS is never a fallback.

reading_support

Hide graphemes during oral segmenting, reveal only taught tiles at build time and keep written directions to one short line.

attention_support

Use the same say-stretch-push-check routine, one target word per screen and no decorative competing motion.

processing_support

Allow unlimited whole-word replay, extra pause time, slower adult-led modelling and a repeat-with-counters option.

confidence_support

Accept non-verbal counter placement, adult-observed oral responses and retries without recording the child's voice.

eal_support

Use familiar pictured vocabulary, confirm the child knows the word meaning first and avoid treating accent differences as phonics errors.
