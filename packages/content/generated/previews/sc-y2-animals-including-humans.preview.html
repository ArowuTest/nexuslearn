Teaching Journey

warm_up

young-adult-feature-compare

Look at the young animal and adult. What has stayed similar, and what has changed?

Activate observation of growth without assuming all offspring resemble adults closely.

Audio: Some young animals look like their adults. Some change a lot as they grow.

Visual: Paired cards show a kitten/cat and caterpillar/butterfly with feature hotspots.

concept_launch

life-stage-card-link

Match offspring to adults, then place the life stages in order.

Establish offspring, growth and adult as life-cycle ideas across familiar animals including humans.

Audio: Offspring grow into adults. The names can change, like chick and hen, or baby and adult.

Visual: Offspring and adult cards connect to a simple two- or three-stage life-cycle strip.

worked_example

need-want-sort-reveal

A rabbit needs water, food, air and shelter. A toy is fun, but it is not a survival need.

Separate basic survival needs from wants and preferences.

Audio: A need is something an animal must have to stay alive. A want can be nice, but it is not the same as a survival need.

Visual: A rabbit card surrounded by need icons; a toy ball moves to a want tray.

guided_practice

body-care-choice-board

Choose one food, one movement and one hygiene action that help a human body.

Connect food variety, exercise and hygiene to body care without moralising foods or bodies.

Audio: A helpful day has different kinds of food, movement that suits the person, and hygiene routines such as washing hands.

Visual: A non-scored daily-care board with food group cards, inclusive movement cards and hygiene action cards.

checkpoint

growth-needs-evidence-link

Which evidence explains why this animal can grow and survive?

Check transfer from life-cycle naming to needs-based reasoning.

Audio: Look for evidence of what the animal needs and how it changes as it grows.

Visual: A simple animal growth sequence sits beside food, water, air, shelter and distractor cards.

teach_back

companion-exercise-variety-repair

Pip says only running fast counts as exercise. Teach Pip a better science answer.

Repair narrow exercise concepts and reinforce inclusive health language.

Audio: Exercise means moving the body so it works harder. People can exercise in different ways.

Visual: Walking, dancing, playground movement, wheelchair sport and stretching cards all connect to a heart-and-muscle effort icon.

offspring-adult-feature-matches

offspring-adult-match intro 320 planned

Match familiar offspring to adults using names and observable features.

Use common examples such as kitten/cat, puppy/dog, lamb/sheep, chick/chicken and baby/adult human; avoid exceptional or distressing images.

life-cycles-with-change

life-cycle-sequence developing 280 planned

Order simple life stages, including animals whose young look different from adults.

Keep metamorphosis examples simple and age appropriate; teacher review must check science precision and image clarity.

survival-needs-not-wants

basic-needs-sort expected 320 planned

Classify air, water, food and shelter as basic needs and separate them from wants or comforts.

Avoid shaming or poverty-coded scenarios; frame wants neutrally as nice or useful but not survival needs.

food-variety-and-body-care

healthy-choice-explain secure 280 planned

Explain that humans need different types and right amounts of food without labelling foods or bodies as good or bad.

Avoid calorie, weight, restriction, dieting and moral labels; include allergy-aware and culturally varied foods.

exercise-and-hygiene-retrieval

healthy-choice-explain retrieval 300 planned

Retain inclusive exercise and hygiene concepts through short evidence-based explanations.

Represent varied bodies, mobility aids and sensory needs respectfully; hygiene examples should reduce germs without making germs frightening.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use calm still images, optional sound, no jump-scare germs, no graphic illness and no competitive exercise effects.

reduced_motion

Replace growth transformations, card movement and germ fade animations with labelled static sequence panels.

audio_first

Narrate offspring names, adult names, need/want cards, food choices, movement options and hygiene steps with replay.

reading_support

Use short prompts, picture cards, symbol-supported because frames and visible vocabulary anchors.

attention_support

Use one concept routine at a time: match-grow-explain, need-or-want, or choice-reason-check.

processing_support

Allow cards to remain visible, reduce choices, provide adult-scribed explanation, undo and unlimited thinking time.

confidence_support

Avoid body shame, food shame, family assumptions and personal health scoring; frame tasks around fictional characters and general science ideas.

eal_support

Preteach offspring, adult, grow, survive, need, water, food, air, shelter, exercise, hygiene and germs with pictures and gestures.
