Teaching Journey

warm_up

object-material-flip

Is the spoon the object or the material?

Check object/material vocabulary before suitability.

Audio: The spoon is the object. Metal is the material.

Visual: Object cards flip to show material labels.

concept_launch

design-lab-roof-test

Choose a material for a rainy-day roof.

Introduce suitability as matching properties to a use.

Audio: A good roof material should keep water out.

Visual: A small shelter waits for a roof material.

worked_example

waterproof-compare

Plastic is suitable for the roof because it is waterproof.

Model because explanation using a property.

Audio: Plastic works because water does not soak through it.

Visual: Raindrops bounce off plastic and soak into paper.

guided_practice

property-station-snap

Sort the materials by flexible, strong or waterproof.

Practise matching properties to material evidence.

Audio: Look at what the material can do.

Visual: Material cards move into property test stations.

checkpoint

transparency-test

Which material is best for a window?

Check property-use reasoning.

Audio: A window needs a material you can see through.

Visual: Glass, fabric and wood cards are tested for transparency.

teach_back

companion-roof-repair

Teach Pip why paper is not the best roof in the rain.

Repair appearance-based choice with property reasoning.

Audio: I like the paper colour. Why should I test it?

Visual: The companion watches paper sag under raindrops.
