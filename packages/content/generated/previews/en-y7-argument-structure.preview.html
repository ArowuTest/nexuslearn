Teaching Journey

warm_up

claim-sort-columns

Sort each sentence as a topic, an opinion or an arguable claim.

Reactivate sentence purpose and distinguish a debatable claim from a broad topic or unsupported preference.

Audio: A useful claim takes a clear position that reasons and evidence can support.

Visual: Sentence cards move into topic, opinion and arguable-claim columns.

concept_launch

argument-route-build

Build the argument route: claim, reason, evidence, counterargument, rebuttal.

Introduce argument as connected reasoning rather than a collection of persuasive phrases.

Audio: A strong argument has a route. Each reason supports the claim, and each piece of evidence supports a reason.

Visual: A clean argument map grows from a central claim into supported branches.

worked_example

evidence-relevance-link

Why is the survey result evidence for extending library opening hours?

Model the difference between a reason, evidence and an explanation of relevance.

Audio: The survey matters because it shows that many pupils need access after the current closing time.

Visual: A claim card connects to a reason, a survey statistic and a relevance sentence.

guided_practice

evidence-strength-scale

Rank the evidence from strongest to weakest for this reason, then explain your top choice.

Practise selecting evidence for relevance, sufficiency and credibility rather than vividness alone.

Audio: Ask whether the evidence directly supports this reason and whether the reader can trust it.

Visual: Evidence cards sit on a relevance-and-reliability scale.

checkpoint

rebuttal-bridge-test

Choose the rebuttal that answers the counterargument fairly without changing the subject.

Check whether learners can respond to an opposing point with precise reasoning.

Audio: A rebuttal should answer the real challenge, not dismiss the person making it.

Visual: Three rebuttal bridges are tested against one counterargument.

teach_back

companion-reasoning-repair

Teach Nova why three facts do not make an argument unless their relevance is explained.

Consolidate the relationship between evidence and reasoning through verbal explanation.

Audio: I found three facts about the topic. What must I add so the reader can follow my reasoning?

Visual: The companion holds disconnected fact cards beside a completed argument route.

argument-map review difficulty 5

Place each sentence in the correct role for an argument about extending the school library's opening hours.

The claim states the position; the reason explains why; the survey supports that reason; the staffing point challenges the proposal; and the trial responds directly to that challenge.

evidence-rank review difficulty 5

Which evidence most strongly supports the reason that a refill station could reduce single-use plastic bottles at school?

The waste audit directly measures the problem the proposal aims to reduce and gives specific school-based evidence.

en-y7-argument-structure-q-build-paragraph

paragraph-build review difficulty 6

Order the sentences into a coherent argument paragraph.

The paragraph moves from claim to reason to evidence, then explains how the evidence supports the claim.

en-y7-argument-structure-q-rebuttal

reason-choice review difficulty 7

A writer argues that the school should publish a monthly pupil newspaper. Which rebuttal best answers the counterargument that printing it would waste paper?

The digital-first proposal accepts the legitimate concern and answers it with a practical way to reduce paper use.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use a calm paper-and-ink board, steady focus outlines and no competitive timers or flashing verdicts.

reduced_motion

Replace connecting and ranking animations with static numbered cards and persistent role labels.

audio_first

Read argument cards aloud by role, with replay and an option to hear the completed reasoning route.

reading_support

Use line focus, adjustable spacing, short card-sized chunks and icons for claim, reason, evidence and response.

attention_support

Reveal one branch at a time and keep the current reasoning question visible while unused cards are dimmed.

processing_support

Allow planning time, card rehearsal and a printable or static outline before paragraph construction.

confidence_support

Credit a sound reason or relevant evidence separately, then offer one precise next repair instead of rejecting the whole argument.

eal_support

Preteach claim, reason, evidence, supports, however, counterargument and rebuttal; provide spoken rehearsal and connective frames.
