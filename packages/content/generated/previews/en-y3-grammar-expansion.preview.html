Teaching Journey

warm_up

noun-verb-anchor-scan

Find the main noun and the verb in each short sentence.

Retrieve the sentence anchors needed before adding phrases and clauses.

Audio: Find who or what the sentence is about, then find the action or state.

Visual: Noun and verb tiles lock into labelled slots without relying on colour.

concept_launch

useful-detail-gates

Expand 'the door' so a reader knows exactly which door, but keep only details that help.

Frame expansion as a meaning choice rather than a contest to make sentences longer.

Audio: Useful detail can identify, describe or explain. Extra words are not always better.

Visual: Candidate detail tiles pass through IDENTIFY, DESCRIBE and REMOVE gates.

worked_example

relationship-bridge-switch

Compare: 'The path flooded because the rain continued' and 'The path flooded although the rain continued.'

Model how conjunction choice changes the relationship between complete ideas.

Audio: Because gives a cause. Although gives an unexpected contrast. The link must match the meaning.

Visual: Two clause blocks stay fixed while the relationship bridge changes label and meaning.

guided_practice

sentence-expansion-stepper

Choose one useful noun detail, add a time or cause phrase, then join a second clause if it improves the message.

Coordinate noun-phrase expansion, phrase placement and clause linking in manageable stages.

Audio: Build, read, and check after each addition. Keep the main message easy to find.

Visual: A three-stage editor shows noun detail, phrase detail and optional clause link separately.

checkpoint

missing-clause-socket

Is 'Because the gate was locked' a complete message here, or does it need another clause?

Diagnose fragments and test whether conjunction-led clauses have a complete partner.

Audio: Because introduces a reason. Ask what happened because the gate was locked.

Visual: The because-clause waits beside an empty main-clause socket.

teach_back

meaning-load-balance

Teach the companion why the clearest sentence is not always the longest one.

Secure editing judgement and resistance to adjective or clause overload.

Audio: Point to each added part, name its job and remove anything that does not help.

Visual: A sentence meter compares useful meaning with reading load, without scoring length.

Question Variants

en-y3-grammar-expansion-q-noun-detail

noun-phrase-builder review difficulty 4

The reader must identify the key that opens the attic. Which noun phrase is most useful?

The size, material and star-shaped bow help the reader identify the particular key; the other choices are vague or ungrammatical.

en-y3-grammar-expansion-q-because-fragment

sentence-editor review difficulty 5

Repair the fragment 'Because the gate was locked.'

The repaired sentence includes the reason and a main clause explaining the result.

en-y3-grammar-expansion-q-although

clause-linker review difficulty 5

The writer wants to show an unexpected contrast. Choose the best link: '___ the path was muddy, the walkers continued.'

'Although' shows that continuing was unexpected in contrast with the muddy conditions.

en-y3-grammar-expansion-q-clarity-edit

sentence-editor review difficulty 7

Which edit keeps the useful meaning clearest?

The edit keeps useful size, place and time information while removing repetition and restoring a clear sentence structure.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use a calm writing desk, stable text, muted highlights and no shaking or red failure effects.

reduced_motion

Replace tile movement and bridge changes with numbered static before-and-after sentence panels.

audio_first

Read each chunk and the complete sentence neutrally; offer slow replay and oral response before written editing.

reading_support

Use adjustable spacing, line focus, clause chunking, short definitions and a persistent noun-verb-conjunction glossary.

attention_support

Follow a fixed FIND MAIN MESSAGE-ADD ONE DETAIL-CHECK MEANING routine with one visible decision at a time.

processing_support

Remove timers, preserve the original sentence, allow undo and provide reduced choices while retaining the same grammatical relationship.

confidence_support

Credit concise writing, preserve correct chunks during repair and describe changes as editing decisions rather than failures.

eal_support

Preteach clause relationships with pictures and paired sentences; allow oral rehearsal and home-language discussion without assuming conjunctions map directly.
