Teaching Journey

warm_up

noun-anchor-reveal

Which word names the thing: the small boat?

Secure the noun as the anchor before adding or removing details.

Audio: Ask who or what the phrase is about. Boat is the noun.

Visual: The noun tile sits in a fixed centre slot while the and small sit in labelled outer slots.

concept_launch

detail-narrows-choice

Change 'the bag' so your reader can find the right bag.

Introduce expansion as useful meaning, not simply adding more words.

Audio: Choose details that help the reader picture or identify the noun.

Visual: Several bag cards differ by colour, size and pattern; selected details narrow the match.

worked_example

phrase-slot-build

Build: the small, spotted dog.

Model determiner, purposeful adjectives, noun order and an age-appropriate comma between two listed adjectives.

Audio: Start with the. Add small and spotted. End with the noun dog. Read the whole phrase to check it sounds right.

Visual: Four labelled tiles occupy DETERMINER, DETAIL, DETAIL and NOUN slots with a comma card between the details.

guided_practice

phrase-into-sentence

Choose one or two details for the noun, build the phrase, then hear it inside a sentence.

Practise natural order, useful selection and oral checking without handwriting demands.

Audio: Build the phrase. Listen to the sentence. Remove any detail that repeats or does not help.

Visual: A phrase builder feeds into a short sentence strip with replay and undo.

checkpoint

missing-verb-slot

Is 'the shiny red bike' a noun phrase or a complete sentence?

Check that expansion does not blur the difference between a noun phrase and a complete sentence.

Audio: The phrase names the bike, but it does not yet tell us what the bike does or is.

Visual: The phrase fills a noun slot while an empty verb slot remains visible.

teach_back

companion-detail-sort

Teach Pip why 'the lovely nice amazing hat' is not always the clearest choice.

Repair adjective piling and secure reader-focused editing.

Audio: Tell Pip which detail helps the reader and which words repeat the same idea.

Visual: Detail cards sort into HELPS THE READER, REPEATS and DOES NOT FIT.

en-y2-expanded-noun-phrases-q-find-noun

phrase-or-sentence review difficulty 2

Which word is the noun in 'the tiny green frog'?

Frog is the noun because it names the animal; tiny and green describe the frog.

en-y2-expanded-noun-phrases-q-useful-bag

useful-detail-choice review difficulty 4

Sam must find the bag with stars. Which phrase helps most?

Blue and with silver stars help the reader identify the particular bag.

en-y2-expanded-noun-phrases-q-build-dog

noun-phrase-builder review difficulty 5

Build a clear phrase for a dog that is small and spotted.

The phrase starts with a determiner, adds two useful details and ends with the noun dog.

en-y2-expanded-noun-phrases-q-complete-sentence

sentence-repair review difficulty 5

Which choice turns 'the shiny red bike' into a complete sentence?

The complete sentence names the bike, tells what it did and includes sentence punctuation.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use a calm writing desk, stable tiles, muted focus outlines and no shaking, red failure marks or animated word clouds.

reduced_motion

Replace tile movement and detail filtering with numbered static phrase stages and before-and-after sentences.

audio_first

Read every word, phrase and completed sentence neutrally; allow oral rehearsal, slow replay and spoken answers before print choices.

reading_support

Offer adjustable spacing, line focus, symbol-backed word roles, short decodable examples and a persistent noun/detail glossary.

attention_support

Use a fixed FIND NOUN-ADD ONE DETAIL-READ-CHECK routine and reveal only the current decision.

processing_support

Allow unlimited time, undo, reduced choices, sentence frames and adult scribing without lowering the language objective.

confidence_support

Accept varied respectful descriptions, credit concise phrases and describe revision as helping the reader rather than fixing bad writing.

eal_support

Preteach noun, describe, detail, phrase and determiner with objects and pictures; allow oral rehearsal and home-language discussion because adjective order may differ across languages.
