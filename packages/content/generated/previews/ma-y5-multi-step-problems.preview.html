Teaching Journey

warm_up

question-target-lock

Read only the question first. What final quantity must the answer describe?

Anchor attention on the required outcome before processing numbers and details.

Audio: Name the final quantity and its unit before choosing any operation.

Visual: The question is isolated above a FINAL ANSWER box with quantity and unit fields.

concept_launch

dependency-map-build

Turn the story into a map: what is known, what must be found first, and what must be found last?

Represent the dependency between steps instead of calculating in text order.

Audio: A plan shows which result unlocks the next step.

Visual: Known quantity cards connect to an intermediate box and then a final-answer box.

worked_example

stock-change-chain

A library had 18,750 books, received 2,680 and removed 1,945. Find the new total by adding the delivery, then subtracting the removed books.

Model a two-step change problem with labelled intermediate working, efficient written methods and an estimate.

Audio: First find the total after the delivery. Then subtract the removed books. Keep each result labelled.

Visual: A stock bar grows for the delivery, shrinks for removals and stays aligned with two calculation cards.

guided_practice

plan-solve-check-console

Build the calculation chain, solve one card at a time and compare the final answer with your estimate.

Coordinate representation, calculation and checking while reducing working-memory load.

Audio: Plan before calculating. Label each answer, then ask whether the final value is sensible.

Visual: A three-panel console shows PLAN, SOLVE and CHECK with only the active panel expanded.

checkpoint

relationship-over-keyword

The problem says 'more', but should the first step add or subtract? Use the quantity diagram to decide.

Detect keyword-led operation choice and replace it with relational reasoning.

Audio: Words can appear in different relationships. Decide what is known and what is missing.

Visual: The same word appears in two comparison bars that require different operations.

teach_back

companion-route-replay

Teach your companion why your step order works and how your estimate checks the result.

Gather explanation evidence for dependency, method choice and reasonableness.

Audio: Explain what each calculation finds, why it comes next and what would make the final answer unreasonable.

Visual: The learner points through a completed route while labels and estimate bounds remain visible.

Question Variants

ma-y5-multi-step-problems-q-library-chain

calculation-chain review difficulty 6

A library had 18,750 books. It received 2,680 books and removed 1,945 damaged books. How many books does it have now?

18,750 + 2,680 = 21,430, then 21,430 - 1,945 = 19,485 books.

ma-y5-multi-step-problems-q-fundraising-map

problem-map review difficulty 8

Year 5 raised £1,875. Year 6 raised £460 more than Year 5. The school spent £925 of the combined amount. How much remained?

Year 6 raised £2,335; together the years raised £4,210; after £925 was spent, £3,285 remained.

ma-y5-multi-step-problems-q-keyword-trap

method-choice review difficulty 7

A tank holds 12,500 litres when full. It currently has 3,850 litres. How many more litres are needed, then how much water will be left if 2,400 litres are used after filling?

12,500 - 3,850 = 8,650 litres are needed. Once full, 12,500 - 2,400 = 10,100 litres remain.

ma-y5-multi-step-problems-q-first-error

error-analysis review difficulty 7

A learner solves: 'A venue sold 4,825 morning tickets and 3,760 afternoon tickets. It refunded 685 tickets. How many tickets remained sold?' They write 4,825 - 3,760 = 1,065, then 1,065 - 685 = 380. Select and repair the first error.

The first step should add the two groups of sold tickets. The correct chain gives 7,900 tickets remaining sold.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use a calm Orbit City planning console, no countdowns, no moving background traffic and one highlighted quantity relationship at a time.

reduced_motion

Replace card travel and growing bars with numbered static panels showing before, operation and after states.

audio_first

Read the question separately from the context, announce quantities with units, and replay the learner's plan as complete operation sentences.

reading_support

Offer sentence-by-sentence reveal, adjustable spacing, a relevant-information view and icons for start, change, compare, combine and final quantity.

attention_support

Use a fixed QUESTION-PLAN-SOLVE-CHECK strip, collapse completed text while preserving labelled data and pause after every intermediate result.

processing_support

Remove timers, allow unlimited plan revisions, keep intermediate results visible, and provide undo without clearing correct later-independent work.

confidence_support

Credit valid planning even after arithmetic errors, frame repair as debugging a route and retain all correct quantity labels.

eal_support

Preteach increase, decrease, difference, total, remaining, intermediate and reasonable with bar models; do not teach single-word operation rules.
