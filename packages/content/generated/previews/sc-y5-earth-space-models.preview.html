Teaching Journey

warm_up

motion-card-compare

Match rotate and orbit to the correct movement.

Check movement vocabulary before modelling.

Audio: Rotate means spin. Orbit means move around something.

Visual: Two motion cards compare spin and orbit.

concept_launch

observatory-model-open

Use the observatory model to test what moves around what.

Introduce model-based reasoning for space movement.

Audio: A model helps us explain movement that is too big to see all at once.

Visual: An orbit table with Sun, Earth and Moon pieces.

worked_example

day-night-rotation

Earth rotates, so one side faces the Sun and the other side has night.

Explain day and night using rotation, not Sun orbiting Earth.

Audio: Day and night happen because Earth spins.

Visual: Earth rotates with a lit side and dark side.

guided_practice

earth-orbit-snap

Drag Earth onto its orbit around the Sun.

Practise relative movement of Earth and Sun.

Audio: Earth travels around the Sun on its orbit.

Visual: Orbit path with draggable Earth model.

checkpoint

moon-reflection-test

Does the Moon make its own light?

Detect Moon-light misconception.

Audio: The Moon does not make its own light. It reflects sunlight.

Visual: Sunlight reflects from Moon toward Earth.

teach_back

companion-day-night-repair

Teach Sol why day and night do not happen because the Sun moves around Earth.

Use teach-back to repair geocentric day/night reasoning.

Audio: I thought the Sun goes around us every day. Can you show me the model?

Visual: The companion watches the Earth rotation model.
