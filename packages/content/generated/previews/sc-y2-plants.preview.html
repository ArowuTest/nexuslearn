Teaching Journey

warm_up

observation-lens-scan

Look closely at the seed and bulb. What can you observe without guessing?

Retrieve observation language and separate visible evidence from assumptions.

Audio: Say what you can see, feel or measure safely. Do not say what is inside unless evidence shows it.

Visual: Large seed and bulb images sit beside SEE, FEEL WITH PERMISSION and MEASURE icons.

concept_launch

growth-stage-order

Put the pictures in order from seed to mature plant.

Establish growth as a gradual sequence with roots and shoots developing over time.

Audio: The first root usually grows down. The shoot grows up. Leaves open as the young plant develops.

Visual: Four dated observation cards show seed, first root, shoot with leaves and mature plant.

worked_example

dated-observation-compare

Compare day 3 and day 7. What changed, and what stayed the same?

Model specific comparative observation using dates, simple measures and plant features.

Audio: On day seven the shoot is taller and has two leaves. It is still the same bean plant.

Visual: Two photo cards align beside a ruler, leaf counter and sentence frame.

guided_practice

one-condition-test

Keep the plants the same, change one condition, then predict and observe.

Introduce simple fair comparison for water, light or temperature.

Audio: Change one thing so we can use the result as evidence about that condition.

Visual: Two matched plants share identical icons except for one highlighted condition card.

checkpoint

germination-versus-health-timeline

A seed sprouted in a dark cupboard. Does that prove the plant never needs light?

Diagnose confusion between early germination and conditions for continued healthy growth.

Audio: A seed can use stored food to begin growing. Watch what happens to the young plant over more time without light.

Visual: A short pale shoot in darkness is compared with later healthy and light-deprived observations.

teach_back

companion-water-evidence

Teach Pip why giving a plant lots and lots of water may not keep it healthy.

Repair the linear more-is-better misconception and secure suitable-condition language.

Audio: Plants need enough water, but too much can stop roots getting the conditions they need.

Visual: Dry, suitable and waterlogged pots display evidence cards rather than happy/sad faces.

Question Variants

sc-y2-plants-q-growth-order

growth-sequence review difficulty 3

Put the bean growth pictures in order.

The seed germinates, a first root emerges, the shoot and leaves develop, and the plant grows towards maturity.

sc-y2-plants-q-observation

observation-record review difficulty 4

Which is the best observation when day 7 is compared with day 3?

The measured height increased from two to six centimetres and two leaves appeared.

sc-y2-plants-q-fair-light-test

plant-needs-test review difficulty 5

Which test gives the clearest evidence about light?

Changing light while keeping the other conditions alike makes the comparison clearer.

sc-y2-plants-q-dark-germination

evidence-choice review difficulty 6

A bean sprouted in darkness but later became pale and weak. Which explanation fits the evidence?

A seed can begin germinating using stored food, while the developing plant needs light to continue growing healthily.

sc-y2-plants-q-too-much-water

fair-comparison review difficulty 5

Which conclusion is best when the waterlogged plant has yellow leaves and a weak stem?

The observations show that excess water did not keep this plant healthy, so plants need suitable conditions rather than the greatest possible amount.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use calm natural images, no distressing decay close-ups, no sudden wilting animation and optional ambient sound.

reduced_motion

Use static dated growth cards and before-and-after condition panels instead of time-lapse, moving roots or wilting effects.

audio_first

Narrate every stage, date, measurement and condition; provide replay and audio descriptions that do not disclose the answer.

reading_support

Pair short sentences with icons, use first-next-later frames, adjustable spacing and a persistent seed-root-shoot-leaf glossary.

attention_support

Show one observation or one changed condition at a time using OBSERVE-COMPARE-EXPLAIN.

processing_support

Allow image zoom, unlimited viewing time, reduced choices, physical card alternatives and adult-supported observation recording.

confidence_support

Treat predictions as ideas to test, preserve correct observations during repair and avoid scoring plant-care outcomes as personal success or failure.

eal_support

Preteach seed, bulb, root, shoot, leaf, light, water, warm, healthy and suitable with real objects or pictures; allow home-language observation before English sentence frames.
