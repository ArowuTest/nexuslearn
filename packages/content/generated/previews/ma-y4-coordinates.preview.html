Teaching Journey

warm_up

origin-compass-trace

Find the origin, then trace across and up to the treasure point.

Secure the origin and the across-then-up coordinate convention.

Audio: Start at zero, zero. Count across on x, then count up on y.

Visual: A first-quadrant grid with a compass bot tracing an L-shaped route from the origin.

concept_launch

coordinate-laser-plot

Plot (4, 3) by counting four across and three up.

Introduce coordinates as ordered pairs that describe exact positions.

Audio: The first number is across. The second number is up. Together they name one point.

Visual: The x-step and y-step light in different patterns before the point lands.

worked_example

point-guide-lines

Read the coordinate of the flag point.

Model reading a plotted point back into an ordered pair.

Audio: Drop a guide line to the x-axis, then across to the y-axis. Say x then y.

Visual: Guide lines project from a point to labelled axes.

guided_practice

translation-arrow-slide

Slide the gem from (2, 1) three squares right and two squares up.

Connect horizontal and vertical movements to changes in coordinate values.

Audio: Moving right increases x. Moving up increases y.

Visual: A gem token moves along arrow tracks while the coordinate updates.

checkpoint

coordinate-swap-compare

Which point is (2, 5), and which is (5, 2)?

Detect reversed-coordinate misconceptions.

Audio: These two points use the same numbers, but the order changes the position.

Visual: Two points pulse with swapped x and y labels.

teach_back

companion-polygon-corner-repair

Rex plotted the last corner in the wrong place. Teach Rex how to complete the rectangle.

Use explanation to reason from existing vertices to a missing polygon point.

Audio: I matched one number but forgot the other side of the rectangle. Can you show me the missing corner?

Visual: A rectangle ghost outline appears after the learner tests the x and y matches.

Question Variants

ma-y4-coordinates-q-plot-4-3

coordinate-plot review difficulty 4

Plot the point (4, 3).

(4, 3) means 4 across and 3 up from the origin.

ma-y4-coordinates-q-read-flag

coordinate-read review difficulty 4

A flag is plotted 2 squares across and 5 squares up. What is its coordinate?

The flag is at (2, 5) because it is 2 across on the x-axis and 5 up on the y-axis.

ma-y4-coordinates-q-translate-gem

movement-translation review difficulty 6

A gem starts at (2, 1). It moves 3 squares right and 2 squares up. Where does it finish?

From (2, 1), three right makes x = 5 and two up makes y = 3, so the gem finishes at (5, 3).

ma-y4-coordinates-q-swap-points

tap-choice review difficulty 3

Which coordinate means 5 across and 2 up?

5 across and 2 up is written (5, 2).

ma-y4-coordinates-q-complete-rectangle

polygon-complete review difficulty 7

Three corners of a rectangle are (1, 1), (5, 1) and (1, 4). Where is the missing corner?

The missing corner must share x = 5 with the right-hand corner and y = 4 with the top-left corner, so it is (5, 4).

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use calm map colours, no flashing treasure effects and optional plain-grid mode.

reduced_motion

Replace sliding animations with static start and end positions plus arrow labels.

audio_first

Read coordinates as 'x equals four, y equals three' and as 'four across, three up'.

reading_support

Use persistent x-axis and y-axis labels, coordinate sentence frames and clear ordered-pair spacing.

attention_support

Present one action at a time: find origin, count across, count up, place point.

processing_support

Allow guide-line toggles, route replay, undo and confirm-before-submit.

confidence_support

Frame swapped coordinates as map clues and reward corrected tracing, not speed.

eal_support

Preteach coordinate, axis, origin, across, up, left, right, translate and vertex with icons.
