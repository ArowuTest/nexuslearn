Teaching Journey

warm_up

reference-arrow-link

Which word links back to 'the volcano'?

Check reference tracking before paragraph editing.

Audio: Some words point back to an earlier noun or idea.

Visual: Pronoun arrows connect to noun cards.

concept_launch

cohesion-thread-build

Use cohesion threads to help the reader follow the ideas.

Introduce cohesion as reader guidance.

Audio: Cohesion is the thread that holds writing together.

Visual: Paragraph cards connect with coloured threads.

worked_example

pronoun-link-lock

The machine overheated. It began to shake.

Model clear pronoun reference.

Audio: It points back to the machine, so the reader knows what shook.

Visual: The pronoun 'It' links back to 'machine'.

guided_practice

link-card-snap

Choose a cohesion device to link these two sentences.

Practise selecting links for meaning, not decoration.

Audio: Choose the link that matches the relationship between ideas.

Visual: Sentence cards and candidate link cards.

checkpoint

ambiguous-pronoun-split

What does 'it' refer to in this sentence?

Detect unclear pronoun reference.

Audio: If the reader cannot tell what 'it' means, the writing needs repair.

Visual: Pronoun arrow splits between two possible nouns.

teach_back

companion-cohesion-repair

Teach Miko why adding 'and then' everywhere does not create strong cohesion.

Repair overuse of simple conjunctions.

Audio: I linked everything with and then. Why does it sound weak?

Visual: The companion replaces repeated links with varied reader signposts.
