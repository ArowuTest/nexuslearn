Teaching Journey

warm_up

question-type-compass

What kind of information does the question ask for: a person, place, time, number, thing or reason?

Focus retrieval on the requested information before searching.

Audio: Name the answer type first. It helps you recognise the right evidence.

Visual: Question words connect to simple answer-type icons without revealing an answer.

concept_launch

journal-search-route

Underline two useful words in the question, then choose where you will scan first.

Establish a purposeful locate routine using keywords, headings and text features.

Audio: Choose important words, predict the useful section, then scan and check the full sentence.

Visual: An explorer field guide shows a contents strip, subheadings, paragraphs, caption and diagram.

worked_example

paraphrase-bridge

The question asks where puffins build nests. The text says they make burrows on grassy cliffs. 'Build nests' and 'make burrows' express related ideas.

Model retrieval when the answer wording is not copied directly from the question.

Audio: Do not stop because one keyword is missing. Read for words with the same or a related meaning.

Visual: Question words connect to a synonym bridge and then to the shortest useful evidence phrase.

guided_practice

retrieval-path-build

Choose the best section, highlight the smallest useful evidence and record a short answer.

Coordinate navigation, evidence precision and answer recording while the source remains visible.

Audio: Check that your highlighted words answer this exact question, then turn them into a clear answer.

Visual: A fixed SEARCH-READ-HIGHLIGHT-ANSWER strip sits beside an accessible non-fiction page.

checkpoint

text-feature-focus

The paragraph does not give the wingspan. Which text feature should you inspect next?

Detect paragraph-only searching and route attention to tables, captions or diagram labels.

Audio: Information can be stated in more than one part of a non-fiction page.

Visual: Main text, fact table, caption and labelled diagram remain in a stable reading order.

teach_back

source-evidence-stamp

Teach your explorer partner why a true fact is not enough unless this text supports the answer.

Confirm source-bound retrieval and distinguish textual evidence from prior knowledge.

Audio: Show the exact evidence and explain how it answers the question.

Visual: A memory bubble and a text evidence card compete for a place in the answer journal.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use cream reading panels, a single static focus outline, no animated page clutter and optional removal of decorative illustrations.

reduced_motion

Replace moving search lenses and feature transitions with persistent numbered steps and user-controlled page changes.

audio_first

Read the question separately, then provide sentence- or feature-level source playback while preserving neutral delivery and visible text.

reading_support

Use adjustable line spacing, sentence masking, syllable-safe vocabulary glosses, heading navigation and phrase-list evidence selection without revealing the answer.

attention_support

Keep question and source visible, reveal one feature at a time and use a fixed FOCUS-LOCATE-READ-ANSWER routine.

processing_support

Remove timers, preserve selected keywords and evidence, allow repeated rereading and accept oral, typed or structured-choice recording.

confidence_support

Treat an incorrect location as a search-route clue, retain useful keywords and separate retrieval knowledge from spelling or handwriting accuracy.

eal_support

Preteach question words and topic vocabulary with visuals, explicitly bridge common synonyms and accept semantically equivalent concise answers.
