forge-array-grow

The forge needs equal rows of energy cells. Equal rows make multiplication faster.

Set multiplication inside a meaningful visual context.

Audio: Equal rows help us see a multiplication fact all at once.

Visual: Rows of cells snap into an array.

worked_example

array-split-recombine

7 x 8 can be split into 5 x 8 and 2 x 8. That is 40 and 16, so the product is 56.

Show decomposition rather than rote-only recall.

Audio: Break seven rows into five rows and two rows. Forty plus sixteen is fifty-six.

Visual: An array separates into 5 rows and 2 rows, then recombines.

guided_practice

array-drag-snap

Build 7 rows of 8, then tap the two parts that help you remember the fact.

Move from visual structure to recall strategy.

Audio: Build it first. Then choose the helpful parts.

Visual: Drag row blocks into an array grid.

checkpoint

route-choice

Which fact helps you check 7 x 8: 5 x 8 plus 2 x 8, or 7 + 8?

Detect additive confusion before independent practice.

Audio: Choose the helper fact that keeps equal groups.

Visual: Two possible routes appear on a control panel.

teach_back

array-rotate-commute

Show your companion why 8 x 7 is the same product as 7 x 8.

Use explanation as retrieval and evidence.

Audio: Can you teach me how swapping the factors keeps the product?

Visual: The array rotates while the product remains fixed.

timed-recall approved difficulty 6

Power the forge: 7 x 8 = ?

7 x 8 is 56 because 5 rows of 8 is 40 and 2 more rows of 8 is 16.

ma-y4-number-multiplication-12x12-q-8x7-choice

multiple_choice approved difficulty 5

Which product matches 8 x 7?

8 x 7 and 7 x 8 both make 56 because the same array can be turned around.

ma-y4-number-multiplication-12x12-q-division-match

division-match approved difficulty 7

The forge made 56 energy cells in 8 equal rows. How many cells are in each row?

56 divided into 8 equal rows gives 7 in each row because 7 x 8 = 56.
