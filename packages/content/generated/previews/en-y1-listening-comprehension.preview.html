Teaching Journey

warm_up

listening-gate-soft-open

Choose your listening plan: still body, quiet hands, eyes where they feel comfortable, replay ready.

Create an inclusive attention routine without demanding eye contact or stillness as proof of listening.

Audio: Get comfortable. You may look at the listening card, look away, move quietly or use replay.

Visual: A choice board shows comfortable listening options and a large replay button.

concept_launch

detail-token-ready

Listen for three treasure tokens: who, what happened and where.

Focus attention on a small, predictable set of explicit details.

Audio: You do not need to remember every word. Listen for who, what and where.

Visual: Three empty picture-token spaces appear before the audio begins.

worked_example

detail-token-place

Listen: 'Mina put on her red boots and splashed through the puddle by the gate.'

Model retrieval from one complete spoken sentence without keyword matching.

Audio: Who? Mina. What? She splashed. Where? By the gate. Red tells us about the boots, but it is not the place.

Visual: Mina, splashing and gate pictures fill the three detail-token spaces after listening.

guided_practice

whole-message-picture-check

Listen once, pause to think, then tap or point to the picture that matches the whole meaning.

Practise retrieval and selection while resisting single-word picture distractors.

Audio: Hear the whole part before choosing. Replay if you need it. Check that every part of the picture fits.

Visual: Three large picture choices appear only after the short audio finishes.

checkpoint

spoken-clue-link

Tari takes an umbrella and the path is shiny. What clue suggests it has rained?

Distinguish a simple text-based inference from guessing or picture knowledge.

Audio: Point to the part you heard that helped you work it out.

Visual: Umbrella and shiny-path clue cards connect to a rain picture.

teach_back

sequence-path-replay

Show Nixi what happened first, next and last, then tell or point to one clue.

Gather sequencing and explanation evidence through speech or accessible non-verbal response.

Audio: Put the three pictures in order. You can tell me, point, tap or ask an adult to say your answer.

Visual: Three large numbered spaces and a clue token remain on screen.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use a quiet Wonder Garden listening nook, plain backgrounds, no music beneath speech and gentle confirmations without surprise sounds.

reduced_motion

Replace moving story elements with static pictures that appear after audio and simple outlines around the selected response.

audio_first

Use produced, human-listened UK narration with whole-text and sentence replay. Browser TTS is prohibited and unavailable audio must show an honest not-ready state.

reading_support

Keep child print optional, use picture choices with spoken labels and show adult transcripts only outside the assessed listening view.

attention_support

Use one short audio text per screen, a visible listen-think-show routine and choices that appear only after listening.

processing_support

Allow unlimited replay, longer pauses, sentence-by-sentence mode, reduced two-choice sets and adult-controlled playback.

confidence_support

Do not score replay count or response speed; accept pointing, tapping, AAC and adult-observed answers without recording the child's voice.

eal_support

Check key story vocabulary and cultural context before assessment, use clear natural narration and avoid treating accent, spoken grammar or unfamiliar background knowledge as comprehension failure.
