Teaching Journey

warm_up

reference-arrow-trace

Follow each reference arrow. What does this, it or they point to?

Activate antecedent tracking before learners edit longer passages.

Audio: A clear reference has one sensible destination.

Visual: Reference words and noun phrases appear on cards connected by selectable arrows.

concept_launch

reader-route-thread

Build a route the reader can follow through repeated ideas, clear references and useful signposts.

Frame cohesion as meaning and reader orientation rather than adding connectives mechanically.

Audio: Good links show what continues, changes, happens next or moves elsewhere.

Visual: A paragraph route uses threads labelled topic, reference, time, place and order.

worked_example

reference-and-time-link

The roof garden collected rainwater. This supply fed the planters. Later that week, the seedlings opened.

Model a precise noun-to-determiner reference and a time adverbial chosen for a genuine shift.

Audio: This supply points clearly to rainwater. Later that week moves the reader forward in time.

Visual: One reference thread and one timeline signpost are added while unnecessary link cards stay unused.

guided_practice

cohesion-editor-build

Order the paragraph cards, connect each reference and edit only the links that help the reader.

Coordinate sequencing, reference clarity, lexical cohesion and adverbial choice.

Audio: Reread after every edit. A link should make the relationship clearer, not merely make the sentence longer.

Visual: A split editor shows paragraph cards, reference arrows and a read-aloud flow check.

checkpoint

ambiguous-arrow-split

Mara placed the model beside the lamp before moving it. What could it mean, and how can the sentence be repaired?

Detect ambiguous pronoun reference and select a proportionate repair.

Audio: If a reference arrow can point to two nouns, repeat the important noun or rewrite the sentence.

Visual: The pronoun arrow splits toward model and lamp until the learner edits the noun phrase.

teach_back

signpost-purpose-test

Teach your companion why firstly, meanwhile and nearby cannot simply swap places.

Gather metalinguistic evidence that cohesive devices encode different relationships.

Audio: Explain whether each signpost shows order, simultaneous time or place.

Visual: Three signposts are tested on the same paragraph gap and mapped to different relationship icons.

Question Variants

en-y5-cohesion-q-reference-map

reference-map review difficulty 4

In the paragraph, what does 'This supply' refer to?

'This supply' refers to the collected rainwater stored in the tank.

en-y5-cohesion-q-ambiguous-it

cohesion-edit review difficulty 5

Repair the unclear reference: 'Mara placed the model beside the lamp before moving it closer to the wall.' Mara moved the model.

Repeating 'the model' removes the ambiguity and tells the reader exactly what Mara moved.

en-y5-cohesion-q-paragraph-signpost

paragraph-order review difficulty 5

Choose the best opening for the second paragraph. Paragraph 1 explains the first safety check. Paragraph 2 explains the next check.

'Secondly' tells the reader that the next paragraph gives the second step in an ordered explanation.

en-y5-cohesion-q-tense-shift

cohesion-edit review difficulty 7

Choose the sentence that clearly shows the discovery happened before the team returned.

'Earlier' and the past perfect 'had discovered' make the earlier event clear while the main narrative remains in the past.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use a calm editing desk, static paragraph cards, one visible thread family at a time and no decorative page movement.

reduced_motion

Replace drawing and snapping threads with persistent numbered reference labels, before/after passage panels and user-controlled focus changes.

audio_first

Read the whole passage neutrally, replay individual paragraphs and announce reference targets and tense changes without supplying the answer.

reading_support

Provide line focus, adjustable spacing, paragraph summaries, optional vocabulary glosses and a text-list alternative to visual thread maps.

attention_support

Use a fixed ORDER-REFERENCE-SIGNPOST-REREAD sequence and reveal only one editing decision while keeping the source passage available.

processing_support

Remove timers, preserve edits during rereading, allow undo and offer oral, typed or structured-choice explanations.

confidence_support

Frame every task as reader testing, preserve effective original wording and show that removing a link can be a successful edit.

eal_support

Preteach reference words and time, place and order adverbials in short paired examples; allow oral rehearsal and discuss when exact noun repetition supports clarity.
