Question Variants

ma-y2-number-add-subtract-two-digit-q-34-plus-18

base-ten-build approved difficulty 5

Build 34 + 18. What is the total?

34 + 18 = 52 because 4 ones plus 8 ones makes 12 ones, which regroup as 1 ten and 2 ones.

ma-y2-number-add-subtract-two-digit-q-46-minus-20

base-ten-build approved difficulty 4

Build 46, then take away 20. What is left?

46 - 20 = 26 because two tens are removed and the six ones stay.

ma-y2-number-add-subtract-two-digit-q-57-minus-9

number-line approved difficulty 6

Use jumps to find 57 - 9.

57 - 9 can be solved by 57 - 7 = 50, then 50 - 2 = 48.

ma-y2-number-add-subtract-two-digit-q-strategy-63-minus-20

tap-choice review difficulty 5

Which strategy is most efficient for 63 - 20?

Removing two tens from 63 gives 43. The three ones stay the same.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use calm workshop lighting and avoid fast spinning gear effects.

reduced_motion

Replace regrouping animations with step-by-step static frames.

audio_first

Read calculations and model changes aloud with optional slower narration.

reading_support

Use number cards, rods and cubes before written word problems.

attention_support

Chunk tasks into build, swap, answer and explain stages.

processing_support

Allow learners to undo block moves and replay the regrouping swap.

confidence_support

Use neutral repair prompts such as 'Let's rebuild the tens and ones'.

eal_support

Preteach add, subtract, total, left, tens, ones and swap with picture anchors.
