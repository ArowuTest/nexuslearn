Teaching Journey

warm_up

source-vibration-scan

Find what is moving when each sound is made.

Activate observation of the sound source rather than guessing from the object name.

Audio: Look for the part that vibrates. That movement is the clue.

Visual: A tuning fork, ruler, drum skin and vocal cords are shown before and after making sound.

concept_launch

vibration-to-sound-wave

Sounds are made when something vibrates.

Establish vibration as the cause of sound using visible, safe examples.

Audio: When the object vibrates, it pushes nearby material back and forth. Those vibrations can reach your ear as sound.

Visual: A slowed-down vibration model links object movement to spreading sound waves.

worked_example

medium-pulse-trace

Trace the vibration journey from the source, through the air, to the ear.

Model sound travel through a medium without overloading learners with advanced wave physics.

Audio: The object vibrates, the air passes the vibration along, and the ear detects it.

Visual: Particle dots pass a gentle pulse from a bell to an ear diagram.

guided_practice

pitch-volume-linked-controls

Change one feature at a time. Does the sound become higher, lower, louder or quieter?

Separate pitch from volume through controlled virtual instrument observations.

Audio: Pitch is high or low. Volume is loud or quiet. Change one thing and record what happens.

Visual: A virtual string, ruler and drum show vibration speed/length and vibration size as evidence.

checkpoint

distance-meter-fade

Move the listener away from the source. What pattern do you notice in the volume reading?

Connect distance from source with sounds becoming fainter.

Audio: As the listener moves farther away, the sound usually becomes fainter.

Visual: A sound-level meter records three distances from the same steady source.

teach_back

pitch-volume-companion-repair

Teach your companion why a high sound is not always a loud sound.

Repair pitch-volume confusion through explanation from evidence.

Audio: I thought high pitch meant loud. Which observation proves they are different?

Visual: Two sounds are compared: high quiet whistle and low loud drum.

Question Variants

sc-y4-sound-q-ruler-vibration

vibration-observation review difficulty 4

A ruler is pulled down and released over the edge of a table. What is making the sound?

The sound is made by the ruler vibrating. When the vibration stops, the sound stops.

sc-y4-sound-q-air-medium

medium-trace review difficulty 5

A bell rings across the room. Which route best explains how the sound reaches your ear?

The vibrating bell passes vibrations through the air. The ear detects those vibrations as sound.

sc-y4-sound-q-short-string-pitch

pitch-volume-sort review difficulty 5

Two identical strings are plucked with the same strength. The shorter string makes a higher sound. Which property changed?

The shorter string makes a higher-pitched sound. Pitch describes how high or low a sound is.

sc-y4-sound-q-strong-drum-volume

evidence-explain review difficulty 6

The same drum is tapped gently, then tapped more strongly. The stronger tap makes a larger vibration trace. What changes?

A stronger tap makes the drum skin vibrate more strongly, so the sound is louder.

sc-y4-sound-q-distance-fainter

distance-sound-model review difficulty 5

A steady buzzer is tested at 1 m, 3 m and 6 m. The meter reading gets smaller as the listener moves away. What pattern does this show?

The same sound source is heard less strongly farther away, so the sound becomes fainter as distance increases.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Keep audio optional, quiet by default and never startling; provide visual vibration traces for every sound.

reduced_motion

Replace wave motion with step-by-step still panels, labelled arrows and user-controlled reveals.

audio_first

Offer narrated prompts and replay, but do not require learners to identify sounds by hearing alone.

reading_support

Use short labels, icons for high/low and loud/quiet, and sentence frames such as 'The ___ vibrates, so ___.'.

attention_support

Lock unused instrument controls and show one variable-change question at a time.

processing_support

Allow pause, replay and table review before choosing; remove speeded listening tasks.

confidence_support

Frame wrong predictions as investigation clues and preserve correct observations during retries.

eal_support

Preteach vibrate, source, medium, ear, pitch, volume, high, low, loud, quiet, near and far with paired visuals.
