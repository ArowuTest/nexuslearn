Question Variants

sc-y6-electricity-q-more-cells-brighter

circuit-variable-lab review difficulty 6

Circuit A has one 1.5 V cell. Circuit B has two matching 1.5 V cells in series. The bulb and wires are the same. Which result is most likely in the safe simulator?

With the same suitable bulb and a closed complete circuit, adding a matching cell in series increases the supplied voltage, so the bulb is likely to be brighter in the simulator.

sc-y6-electricity-q-open-switch-reason

evidence-explain-choice review difficulty 5

A circuit has two cells, a buzzer and a switch. The buzzer is silent when the switch is open. What is the best reason?

An open switch creates a gap in the circuit. The buzzer works only when the path is complete and the components are suitable.

sc-y6-electricity-q-symbol-cell

symbol-diagram-build review difficulty 4

Choose the recognised symbol for a cell in a circuit diagram.

A cell is represented by one long line and one short line. The symbol shows the component clearly without drawing the real object.

sc-y6-electricity-q-evidence-table-brightness

component-output-table review difficulty 7

Use the table to choose the best explanation.

The table shows the same switch state but a different number of matching cells. That evidence supports the explanation that the extra cell is linked to the brighter bulb.

sc-y6-electricity-q-safety-unknown-power

evidence-explain-choice review difficulty 5

Nova wants to test whether a bulb gets brighter using an unknown battery pack from home. What should they do?

Circuit investigations must be safe. Learners should not test unknown batteries, mains electricity or loose wires; they should use the simulator or equipment approved by an adult teacher.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Keep the lab visually calm; avoid sparks, shocks, alarms, flashing bulbs or sudden buzzer sounds.

reduced_motion

Replace animated current traces and symbol morphs with static numbered path panels and step buttons.

audio_first

Narrate circuit state, changed variable, meter readings and symbol names, with replay for every prompt.

reading_support

Use short explanation stems, labelled icons and because/therefore sentence frames before open writing.

attention_support

Limit the tray to task-relevant cells and components; reveal distractors only in secure and stretch tasks.

processing_support

Allow learners to test one variable at a time, freeze the circuit, and inspect table rows with row highlighting.

confidence_support

Frame errors as debugging evidence: 'The circuit is giving us a clue' rather than 'wrong'.

eal_support

Preteach voltage, cell, series, symbol, component, switch, brighter, louder, dim, silent, safe and variable.

motor_support

Offer keyboard-accessible symbol placement, snap-to-grid components, undo, and no timed drag requirement.

colour_vision_support

Do not rely on red/green or brightness alone; pair output changes with labels, meters and pattern fills.
