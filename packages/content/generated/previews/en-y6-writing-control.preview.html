Question Variants

en-y6-writing-control-q-register-report

register-slider review difficulty 5

Choose the best version for a formal report.

The best choice is formal, precise and clear while keeping the original meaning.

en-y6-writing-control-q-dash-emphasis

punctuation-purpose review difficulty 6

Choose the punctuation that best emphasises the surprising result: 'The team searched every room ___ the trophy was under the stage.'

A dash creates a strong pause before the surprising result: the trophy was under the stage.

en-y6-writing-control-q-overloaded-sentence

sentence-control-panel review difficulty 7

Which edit controls the sentence most clearly?

The best edit separates the ideas clearly and keeps the cause of the hikers turning back.

en-y6-writing-control-q-tone-precision

effect-edit review difficulty 5

Choose the word that creates a calm, precise tone: 'The guide ___ the pupils to the exit.'

'Directed' is precise and calm. The other verbs change the tone or meaning.

en-y6-writing-control-q-semi-colon-purpose

punctuation-purpose review difficulty 8

Which sentence uses the semicolon most appropriately?

Both sides are complete and closely related: the generator failure explains the flickering lights.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use a calm writing desk, gentle reader-effect meters and no flashing correction states.

reduced_motion

Replace moving sliders, sentence untangling and punctuation snaps with static comparison panels.

audio_first

Read each sentence option aloud with modelled pauses and tone contrast.

reading_support

Use line focus, chunked long sentences, glossary popovers and optional simplified context cards.

attention_support

Ask one control question at a time: audience, purpose, meaning, then effect.

processing_support

Allow unlimited rereads, side-by-side sentence comparison and sentence stems for explaining effect.

confidence_support

Treat editing as professional writer behaviour and credit precise improvement even when terminology is developing.

eal_support

Preteach register, formal, informal, tone, precise, emphasis and audience with examples and non-examples.
