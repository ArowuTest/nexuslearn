Teaching Journey

warm_up

feature-chip-focus

Choose only features you can observe or describe from the organism cards.

Separate evidence-based features from guesses, habitat labels and personal preferences.

Audio: Scientists classify living things using evidence. Start with features you can observe or describe clearly.

Visual: Organism cards with feature chips such as backbone, wings, leaf veins, spores and body segments.

concept_launch

branching-key-route

Follow the yes-or-no questions to classify the mystery organism.

Introduce a branching key as a structured route through observable evidence.

Audio: A good branching key question splits the evidence clearly. It should not ask you to guess the name too soon.

Visual: Large yes/no branching route with one highlighted question at a time.

worked_example

evidence-group-lock

Use evidence to decide whether this animal belongs with vertebrates or invertebrates.

Model reasoned classification using a specific feature rather than appearance alone.

Audio: We are not sorting by cute, scary or where it lives. We are checking whether the evidence shows a backbone.

Visual: X-ray style backbone evidence card compared with a body-segment evidence card.

guided_practice

key-question-test

Build two clear questions that separate these four living things.

Practise designing classification questions that split groups fairly and visibly.

Audio: Try a question that each organism can answer yes or no. If all cards go the same way, the question does not help yet.

Visual: Four organism cards route through two editable yes/no question nodes.

checkpoint

microbe-claim-filter

Which statement is careful and scientific about micro-organisms?

Repair overgeneralised claims about micro-organisms.

Audio: Micro-organisms are living things that are usually too small to see without a microscope. They are not all the same.

Visual: Helpful yeast, decomposer mould and harmful bacteria cards with caution labels.

teach_back

companion-feature-repair

Teach Nova why 'it lives in water' may not be enough evidence for a scientific group.

Consolidate classification reasoning and evidence quality.

Audio: I sorted these together because they all live in water. What evidence should I check next?

Visual: Companion shows fish, duckweed and pond snail; feature evidence overlays appear after feedback.

Question Variants

sc-y6-classification-q-key-route-snail

classification-key review difficulty 5

Use the key route for the pond snail. Does the evidence show a backbone?

The key asks about a backbone. The pond snail evidence shows no backbone, so the route goes to invertebrate evidence.

sc-y6-classification-q-build-question

key-builder review difficulty 6

Which question best separates an oak tree, fern, mushroom and yeast?

Can it make seeds? is an observable biological question that can separate some plant and fungus examples more usefully than opinions or picture colour.

sc-y6-classification-q-feature-sort

feature-sort review difficulty 5

Sort the evidence card 'has a backbone' into the best classification use.

Backbone evidence is a specific characteristic used to separate vertebrates from invertebrates.

sc-y6-classification-q-microbe-claim

claim-evidence-explain review difficulty 6

Which claim is most careful about micro-organisms?

The careful claim names scale and avoids saying all micro-organisms have the same effect.

sc-y6-classification-q-evidence-repair

claim-evidence-explain review difficulty 7

Nova grouped a fish, duckweed and pond snail because they all live in water. What should Nova check next?

Living in water does not make these living things the same biological group. Nova should check specific observable characteristics.

feature-sort intro 360 planned

Classify organisms using observable features such as backbone, leaf structure, body segments, spores and flowering.

Avoid culturally narrow organism sets; use non-colour evidence and accurate but age-appropriate features.

Follow yes/no questions to identify or classify mystery organisms.

Questions must be answerable from evidence shown and should not rely on specialist taxonomy beyond Year 6.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use calm field-guide cards, no surprise reveals and non-alarming micro-organism examples.

reduced_motion

Show static key breadcrumbs and card-group snapshots instead of animated branching.

audio_first

Read organism names, evidence chips and yes/no questions with replay controls.

reading_support

Use simple-text feature chips, labelled icons and picture-supported vocabulary cards.

attention_support

Expose one key question at a time and hide unused branches until needed.

processing_support

Allow side-by-side comparison of two organisms before the full sort.

confidence_support

Frame key-building as test-and-revise so learners can improve rules without feeling wrong.

eal_support

Preteach classify, characteristic, organism, micro-organism, evidence and key with visual glossary cards.
