Teaching Journey

warm_up

sentence-sense-slot

Replace the missing word in 'The puppy was so ___ that it bounded around the garden' and explain what the sentence tells you.

Reactivate sentence-level sense checking before introducing unfamiliar vocabulary.

Audio: Read the whole sentence, predict the kind of meaning needed, then test a word in the gap.

Visual: A sentence strip has a blank linked to energetic actions in the rest of the sentence.

concept_launch

word-detective-journal

Open the explorer journal and collect three clues for the word 'reluctant'.

Introduce vocabulary solving as an evidence-based process rather than a single guess.

Audio: First predict, then collect clues, test a meaning and reread the sentence.

Visual: A journal page links the target word to action, contrast and nearby explanation clues.

worked_example

word-parts-context-lock

In 'The path was impassable, so the hikers turned back', use the word parts and the result to explain 'impassable'.

Model how morphology proposes a meaning and sentence context confirms it.

Audio: Pass gives a starting idea, im and able change it, and turning back confirms that the path could not be passed.

Visual: Impassable is segmented beside an arrow from blocked path to hikers turning back.

guided_practice

dictionary-definition-test

The word 'current' has several meanings. Choose the definition that fits a river sentence and prove it with one clue.

Teach navigation of multiple-meaning dictionary entries and contextual verification.

Audio: Check the word class and numbered meanings, then reread the sentence with your chosen definition.

Visual: A short dictionary entry sits beside a river sentence with one clue phrase highlighted.

checkpoint

phrase-meaning-compare

What does 'a blanket of mist covered the field' mean here, and which detail rules out a real blanket?

Detect literal word-by-word interpretation and unsupported figurative guesses.

Audio: Explain the whole phrase, then point to the context that supports your explanation.

Visual: Literal and contextual meaning cards are tested against a misty field description.

teach_back

vocabulary-routine-stamp

Teach your companion how to solve an unfamiliar word without guessing.

Gather explanation evidence for a reusable context-morphology-dictionary routine.

Audio: Say your first prediction, name the strongest clue, test the meaning and reread to check.

Visual: Four journal tabs read PREDICT, FIND CLUES, TEST and REREAD.

Question Variants

en-y3-vocabulary-q-reluctant

context-clue-map review difficulty 4

In 'Ari was reluctant to step onto the rope bridge. He stayed beside the post until his friends encouraged him', what does 'reluctant' mean?

Ari hangs back and needs encouragement, so 'reluctant' means not willing or unsure about doing something.

en-y3-vocabulary-q-reluctant-substitute

meaning-substitute review difficulty 4

Which replacement keeps the meaning of 'reluctant' in 'Ari was reluctant to step onto the rope bridge'?

'Unsure about' preserves the meaning of 'reluctant' in this context.

en-y3-vocabulary-q-impassable

morphology-context-check review difficulty 5

In 'The path was impassable after the landslide, so the walkers turned back', choose the best meaning and the clue that confirms it.

The word parts suggest 'not able to be passed', and the walkers turning back confirms that meaning.

en-y3-vocabulary-q-current

dictionary-entry-check review difficulty 5

In 'The river's current carried the leaf downstream', which dictionary meaning of 'current' fits?

The sentence describes moving river water carrying a leaf, so the first definition fits.

en-y3-vocabulary-q-blanket-of-mist

phrase-meaning-explain review difficulty 6

What does 'a blanket of mist covered the field' mean in this description?

The phrase compares thick, spreading mist with a blanket covering something; the hidden trees support that meaning.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use a calm journal page, one target word and clue set at a time, optional removal of decorative illustrations and no pulsing text.

reduced_motion

Replace moving clue threads and dictionary page turns with persistent numbered steps and user-controlled reveals.

audio_first

Read the whole passage before isolating the target word, replay each sentence independently and speak dictionary definitions without signalling the answer.

reading_support

Provide line focus, adjustable spacing, syllable-safe pronunciation, phrase-list evidence selection and short definitions while preserving the original source.

attention_support

Keep the target word and sentence visible, limit meaning choices and use a fixed PREDICT-CLUE-TEST-REREAD strip.

processing_support

Remove timers, preserve selected clues, allow repeated rereading and accept oral, typed, synonym or structured explanations.

confidence_support

Treat a first meaning as a testable prediction, retain useful clues during repair and separate vocabulary understanding from decoding, spelling and handwriting.

eal_support

Preteach context, clue, root, definition, synonym and phrase with examples; allow home-language discussion and then bridge to the English evidence phrase.
