Question Variants

ma-y6-reasoning-q-counterexample-multiple

counterexample-hunt review difficulty 6

A pupil says, 'Every multiple of 4 is also a multiple of 8.' Which counterexample proves the claim is false?

12 is a multiple of 4 because 4 x 3 = 12, but it is not a multiple of 8. So the always claim is false.

ma-y6-reasoning-q-fraction-reason-chain

reason-chain review difficulty 6

Choose the best reason to justify that 3/4 is greater than 2/3.

Converting to twelfths gives 9/12 and 8/12. Since 9 twelfths is more than 8 twelfths, 3/4 is greater.

ma-y6-reasoning-q-always-sometimes

claim-tester review difficulty 7

Classify the claim: 'When you multiply a whole number by 10, the answer is always even.'

A whole number multiplied by 10 gives a multiple of 10. Every multiple of 10 is even, so the claim is always true.

ma-y6-reasoning-q-proof-sort-area

proof-sort review difficulty 8

Claim: Rectangles with the same area always have the same perimeter. Explanation: A 3 by 4 rectangle has area 12 and perimeter 14, but a 2 by 6 rectangle has area 12 and perimeter 16. How strong is this explanation?

The two rectangles have the same area but different perimeters, so they are a counterexample to the always claim.

ma-y6-reasoning-q-answer-reason

reason-chain review difficulty 5

A number is divisible by 3 if the sum of its digits is divisible by 3. Which reason shows that 4,218 is divisible by 3?

The digit sum is 15, and 15 is divisible by 3, so 4,218 is divisible by 3.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use calm lab visuals, soft validation states and no failure buzzers for disproved claims.

reduced_motion

Replace moving gates and cracking badges with static before-and-after proof cards.

audio_first

Read claims, domains and explanations aloud with pauses between reasoning steps.

reading_support

Chunk long claims, bold only the condition words and provide glossary popovers for prove, justify and counterexample.

attention_support

Show one reasoning action at a time: understand the claim, test, decide, explain.

processing_support

Allow scratch examples, untimed checking and persistent display of the original claim.

confidence_support

Celebrate finding a false claim as successful reasoning, not as getting maths wrong.

eal_support

Preteach always, sometimes, never, prove, claim, because, therefore and counterexample with visual sentence frames.
