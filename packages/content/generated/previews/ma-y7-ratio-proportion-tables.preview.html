Teaching Journey

warm_up

ratio-counter-build

Build a 2:3 ratio with red and blue counters.

Check ratio as comparison before scaling.

Audio: Two red for every three blue shows a ratio of two to three.

Visual: Counter groups snap into ratio pairs.

concept_launch

ratio-table-scale

Use the ratio table to scale both parts together.

Introduce multiplicative scaling in table form.

Audio: In a ratio table, every part is multiplied by the same scale factor.

Visual: A two-row ratio table with multiplier arrows.

worked_example

scale-lens-both-parts

If 2:3 is scaled by 4, it becomes 8:12.

Model scaling both parts by the same factor.

Audio: Multiply both parts by four: two becomes eight and three becomes twelve.

Visual: Multiplier lens applies to both ratio rows.

guided_practice

missing-ratio-cell

Complete the missing value in the ratio table.

Practise finding missing proportional values.

Audio: Find the scale factor, then use it on the matching part.

Visual: Ratio table with one missing cell and arrow clues.

checkpoint

ratio-total-parts

In a 2:3 ratio, how many total parts are there?

Check part-part versus part-whole understanding.

Audio: Two parts and three parts make five parts altogether.

Visual: Two red bars and three blue bars combine into five total parts.

teach_back

companion-ratio-repair

Teach Nova why adding 4 to both parts is not the same as scaling by 4.

Repair additive reasoning trap.

Audio: I added four to both numbers. Why did the ratio change?

Visual: The companion compares additive and multiplicative tables.
