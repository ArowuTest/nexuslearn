Teaching Journey

warm_up

measure-benchmark-card-reveal

Which known measure helps you estimate: a finger width, a metre stick, a full cup or one hour?

Activate benchmarks so estimates are reasoned rather than guesses.

Audio: Think of something you know. A centimetre is small, a metre is much longer, and one hour is sixty minutes.

Visual: Four benchmark cards appear beside a mystery measure, with audio labels and no scoring timer.

concept_launch

tool-unit-match-snap

Choose the tool and unit for each job: length, mass, capacity, temperature, money or time.

Establish that useful measurement begins by matching the question to the right tool and unit.

Audio: Ask what you are measuring first. Then choose a tool that measures that thing.

Visual: A ruler, balance scale, measuring jug, thermometer, coin tray and clock are matched to labelled task cards.

worked_example

zero-start-interval-highlight

Read the scale from zero. Count the intervals, not just the little lines.

Model careful ruler and simple scale reading with attention to start point and interval size.

Audio: Start at zero. Each space is one centimetre. The pencil ends at twelve centimetres.

Visual: A pencil aligned to a ruler zero; later, a jug and thermometer show labelled intervals.

guided_practice

comparison-symbol-bridge

Measure two objects, then choose >, < or = and say the unit.

Link accurate measurement to comparison language and symbolic recording.

Audio: Read both measures with their units. Then choose the symbol that matches the comparison.

Visual: Two measured objects or containers sit beside a comparison sentence with a unit badge.

checkpoint

coin-value-clock-minute-toggle

Make the same amount of money in a different way, then build the clock time.

Check transfer to value equivalence and time to five minutes without relying on real-world shopping fluency.

Audio: Coins can look different and still have the same value. On a clock, the long minute hand tells the minutes.

Visual: A UK coin tray and an analogue clock face appear as separate low-clutter boards.

teach_back

companion-zero-start-repair

Pip measured from the end of the ruler, not zero. Teach Pip how to fix it.

Consolidate fair measurement, tool use and explanation through repair.

Audio: I put the pencil at the edge of the ruler. What should I line it up with?

Visual: A misaligned pencil is repaired by sliding its start to zero; the measure updates only after the repair.

Question Variants

ma-y2-measures-q-tool-unit-pencil

measure-tool-select review difficulty 3

You want to measure the length of a pencil. Which tool and unit would you choose?

A ruler measures length, and centimetres are an appropriate unit for a pencil.

ma-y2-measures-q-ruler-zero

scale-read review difficulty 4

The pencil starts at 0 cm and ends at 12 cm. How long is it?

The pencil starts at 0 cm and reaches 12 cm, so it is 12 cm long.

ma-y2-measures-q-compare-capacity

measure-compare-symbol review difficulty 5

Jug A holds 600 ml. Jug B holds 450 ml. Choose the symbol that makes the sentence true.

Six hundred millilitres is greater than four hundred and fifty millilitres, so 600 ml > 450 ml.

ma-y2-measures-q-money-same-value

coin-combination-build review difficulty 5

Which coin set has the same value as 50p?

The set with 20p, 20p and 10p has a total value of 50p.

ma-y2-measures-q-quarter-to

clock-face-build review difficulty 6

Build quarter to 3 on the clock.

Quarter to 3 is 15 minutes before 3 o'clock, so the minute hand is on 9 and the hour hand is nearly at 3.

tool-unit-choice-and-estimate

measure-tool-select intro 340 planned

Choose appropriate tools and units for practical Year 2 measurement tasks and make sensible estimates.

Keep contexts culturally broad and school-safe; estimates should be plausible ranges, not exact hidden answers.

read-clear-standard-scales

scale-read developing 340 planned

Read rulers, simple weighing scales, measuring vessels and thermometers to appropriate labelled or countable units.

Avoid ambiguous perspective, hidden starts and non-Year-2 increments; every answer must include the unit.

compare-order-and-record-measures

measure-compare-symbol expected 300 planned

Compare and order measured quantities using >, < and = with consistent units.

Do not compare unlike units without conversion; make visual appearance a plausible but not misleading distractor.

money-values-and-combinations

coin-combination-build secure 300 planned

Recognise pounds and pence, combine coins to a target value and find different combinations for the same amount.

Use current UK denominations, avoid adult purchasing assumptions, and keep pounds/pence notation unambiguous.

time-to-five-minutes-and-intervals

clock-face-build retrieval 320 planned

Tell, make and sequence times to five minutes, including quarter past/to and simple intervals.

Use accessible text equivalents and clear hour-hand placement; avoid contexts requiring knowledge of individual home routines.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use plain measuring boards, quiet confirmation sounds, no pouring splashes, no ticking clock audio and one tool at a time.

reduced_motion

Replace liquid fills, pointer sweeps, coin movement and clock-hand rotations with static before-and-after states and labelled stages.

audio_first

Read tools, units, scale values, coin values, comparison symbols and clock times aloud with replay.

reading_support

Use short prompts, consistent unit badges, symbol narration and picture cues; written notation is paired with audio and objects.

attention_support

Use a fixed choose-tool, estimate, measure, record, check routine with unused controls hidden.

processing_support

Allow magnified scales, stepwise mark counting, auto-align, undo, reduced choices and unlimited thinking time.

confidence_support

Separate motor placement from mathematical judgement, preserve correct selections during repair and never score speed.

eal_support

Preteach length, height, mass, capacity, temperature, value, estimate, unit, scale, greater than, less than, quarter past and quarter to with objects and gestures.
