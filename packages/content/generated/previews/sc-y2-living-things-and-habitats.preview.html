Teaching Journey

warm_up

evidence-lens-reveal

Look closely at the mystery item. What can you observe, and what can you not yet know?

Separate observation from unsupported classification.

Audio: Name visible evidence first. A still picture may not show every sign of life.

Visual: A seed, toy insect and leaf shown one at a time with evidence icons hidden.

concept_launch

life-history-sort

Sort living, dead and never alive. Give one reason for each choice.

Establish life history, not movement or appearance, as the classification basis.

Audio: Ask: is it alive now, was it once alive, or has it never been alive?

Visual: Three labelled trays with a life-history timeline.

worked_example

life-history-sequences

A dormant seed may look still. A wooden spoon was made from something once living.

Address difficult boundary cases using age-appropriate evidence.

Audio: Still does not always mean dead. Wood came from a living tree, but the spoon is not alive.

Visual: A seed germination sequence and tree-to-wood-to-spoon sequence.

checkpoint

microhabitat-condition-overlay

Why might a woodlouse live under a damp log?

Check habitat suitability through conditions and needs rather than visual matching.

Audio: The log gives shelter and the damp place helps the woodlouse avoid drying out.

Visual: A log microhabitat with moisture, shade, shelter and food evidence toggles.

guided_practice

habitat-to-chain-link

Choose a suitable habitat, then build a food chain found there.

Connect organisms, habitat resources and feeding relationships.

Audio: Check what each organism needs and what each consumer eats.

Visual: Habitat cards feed a three-position chain builder.

teach_back

food-arrow-label-reveal

Teach Pip what the arrow means in grass to snail to thrush.

Confirm food-chain order and energy-transfer meaning.

Audio: The arrow points from the food to the living thing that eats it.

Visual: Each arrow gains a 'is eaten by' label when selected.

Question Variants

sc-y2-living-things-and-habitats-q-seed

life-status-sort review difficulty 5

A dry seed is not moving. Which evidence best helps decide whether it is living?

A dormant seed is living because it can germinate and grow when it receives suitable conditions.

sc-y2-living-things-and-habitats-q-spoon

life-status-sort review difficulty 4

A wooden spoon is not alive. Which group best describes its material's history?

The spoon is not living, but its wood came from a tree that was once living.

sc-y2-living-things-and-habitats-q-woodlouse

habitat-evidence-map review difficulty 5

Why is the space under a damp log a suitable microhabitat for a woodlouse?

The damp, sheltered conditions help meet a woodlouse's needs.

sc-y2-living-things-and-habitats-q-chain

food-chain-build review difficulty 6

Build the food chain and choose what each arrow means.

Grass is eaten by the snail, and the snail is eaten by the thrush, so the arrows point from food to eater.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use still habitat scenes, optional ambient sound and no sudden predator animations.

reduced_motion

Replace germination, habitat and feeding animations with labelled before/after panels.

audio_first

Narrate item descriptions, evidence cards and arrow meanings with replay while keeping text visible.

reading_support

Use short evidence statements, consistent icons and one classification or relationship per screen.

attention_support

Follow observe-evidence-decide-explain and needs-conditions-match routines with unused controls hidden.

processing_support

Allow evidence cards to remain open, unlimited thinking time, undo and side-by-side comparisons.

confidence_support

Offer 'not enough evidence yet', preserve correct reasoning and avoid graphic death or predation imagery.

eal_support

Preteach living, dead, never alive, habitat, shelter, damp, producer and eaten by with pictures and gestures.
