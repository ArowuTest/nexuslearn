Teaching Journey

warm_up

say-suggest-sort

Sort each detail: does the text say it directly, or does the detail suggest it?

Separate retrieval from inference before introducing justification.

Audio: Some answers are stated. Other answers are worked out from clues.

Visual: Detail cards move into SAY and SUGGEST columns with persistent text references.

concept_launch

clue-inference-path

Follow the path: notice an action or word, infer what it suggests, then point back to the clue.

Establish inference as a reversible clue-to-idea reasoning chain.

Audio: The clue leads to an idea, and the idea must still fit the clue.

Visual: Three fixed cards read CLUE, INFERENCE and BECAUSE, joined by directional arrows.

worked_example

action-to-motive-link

Tariq folded the note into a tiny square and pushed it deep into his pocket. His action suggests he wants to hide the note.

Model a motive inference from precise action evidence while avoiding unsupported emotion labels.

Audio: Pushing the note deep into his pocket is the clue. It suggests a hiding motive because he puts it out of sight.

Visual: Only the action phrase is highlighted; a motive card connects with a spoken because explanation.

guided_practice

evidence-link-build

Choose a plausible inference, highlight the smallest useful clue, and complete the because link.

Coordinate inference, evidence precision and explanation with scaffolded choices.

Audio: Choose an idea that fits, then highlight only the words that help prove it.

Visual: An extract remains visible above inference cards, a keyboard highlighter and a because sentence frame.

checkpoint

support-balance-test

Which answer fits the clue without claiming more than the text shows?

Detect unsupported certainty and plausible-but-textless guessing.

Audio: Strong readers say what the clue supports, not everything that might be possible.

Visual: Three inference cards sit on evidence blocks; unsupported claims tip away.

teach_back

two-plausible-paths

Teach your companion how two inferences can both be plausible if each one uses a different clue.

Gather oral reasoning evidence and prevent one-word-answer rigidity.

Audio: Show me the clue for each idea and explain why both could fit.

Visual: Two inference paths branch from separate highlighted phrases in the same extract.

clue-highlight review difficulty 4

Highlight the words that best suggest Ava is uncertain.

en-y4-reading-inference-q-motive-choice

inference-choice review difficulty 5

Why does Tariq push the note deep into his pocket?

Pushing the note deep into his pocket when Rosa looks and changing the subject suggest that Tariq wants to hide it from her.

en-y4-reading-inference-q-because-link

evidence-link review difficulty 6

Complete the explanation: Nia may feel relieved because...

Finding the key solves the problem, and Nia's long breath and dropped shoulders suggest tension has left her.

en-y4-reading-inference-q-overclaim

short-response review difficulty 7

Improve this answer so it says only what the clue supports: 'Milo is always dishonest.'

The action supports a possible hiding motive in this moment, but it does not prove that Milo is always dishonest.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use static cream text panels, one non-flashing outline highlight and no animated character faces or ambient movement.

reduced_motion

Replace moving clue paths and balance effects with persistent numbered arrows and supported/unsupported labels.

audio_first

Offer human-paced read-aloud by sentence, replay the question separately and pause after dialogue punctuation without adding interpretive emotion.

reading_support

Use 40-100 word extracts, adjustable line spacing, sentence masking, vocabulary glosses that do not reveal the inference and a phrase-list selection alternative.

attention_support

Keep the source text visible, present one decision at a time and use a fixed CLUE-INFERENCE-BECAUSE progress strip.

processing_support

Remove timers, retain selected evidence while the learner composes, and allow oral, typed or structured-choice explanations.

confidence_support

Describe alternatives as more or less supported, preserve plausible thinking during repair and model that two answers can fit when both have evidence.

eal_support

Preteach feeling, action and motive vocabulary with short scenarios; permit oral rehearsal and accept semantically equivalent evidence explanations.
