Teaching Journey

warm_up

whole-group-count-highlight

Count these pairs, fives and tens. Which count pattern helps?

Retrieve step counting while keeping each count linked to a visible equal group.

Audio: Touch one whole group for each count. Two, four, six. Five, ten, fifteen.

Visual: Two, five or ten counters sit inside clearly outlined groups; one group highlights per spoken count.

concept_launch

equal-groups-fill-check

Make 4 equal groups of 2. Check every group before finding the total.

Establish equal group size as the defining structure for early multiplication.

Audio: Four groups of two means four groups, with two in each one.

Visual: Four labelled hoops receive two counters each and connect to 2 + 2 + 2 + 2 = 8.

worked_example

array-quarter-turn-conserve

Turn 3 rows of 5. What stays the same, and what changes?

Model multiplication commutativity through conserved array quantity.

Audio: Three groups of five and five groups of three both show fifteen dots. The rows change, but the total does not.

Visual: A 3 by 5 array rotates beside the equations 3 x 5 = 15 and 5 x 3 = 15.

guided_practice

division-two-meanings-toggle

Use 20 counters to show 20 shared into 5 equal groups, then show groups of 5.

Compare sharing division and grouping division using the same total.

Audio: Share one at a time to make five equal groups. Then reset and make groups with five in each.

Visual: A total tray switches between five destination trays and a repeating five-counter group frame.

checkpoint

array-fact-family-link

The array shows 5 groups of 2. Choose the multiplication and division facts that match.

Check whether the learner connects one equal-group model to related multiplication and division facts.

Audio: Name the number of groups, the amount in each group and the total.

Visual: A 5 by 2 array sits above selectable equation cards with the total and factors labelled.

teach_back

companion-factor-add-repair

Teach Pip why 3 groups of 4 is not 3 + 4.

Secure the meaning of multiplication and repair factor-addition errors.

Audio: Show each group, say the repeated addition, then tell Pip the total.

Visual: Three four-counter groups appear beside 4 + 4 + 4 and a crossed-out 3 + 4 comparison.

Question Variants

ma-y2-multiplication-foundations-q-equal-groups

equal-groups-builder review difficulty 3

Build 4 equal groups of 2. How many counters are there altogether?

Four equal groups with two in each group contain eight counters altogether.

ma-y2-multiplication-foundations-q-array-turn

array-turn review difficulty 4

This array has 3 rows of 5. Which fact also has the same total?

Three rows of five and five rows of three both contain fifteen dots.

ma-y2-multiplication-foundations-q-share

share-group-divide review difficulty 5

Share 20 counters equally into 5 trays. How many go in each tray?

Sharing twenty equally into five groups gives four in each group because five groups of four make twenty.

ma-y2-multiplication-foundations-q-odd-even

odd-even-pair review difficulty 4

Pair 15 counters. Is 15 odd or even?

Fifteen is odd because making pairs leaves one counter without a partner.

Adaptive Support

low_sensory

Use a plain counting mat, muted outlines, optional sound effects and no bouncing counters or countdowns.

reduced_motion

Replace counter movement, array turns and sharing animation with numbered static build stages and before-and-after panels.

audio_first

Read group count, group size, symbols and equations aloud; describe arrays by rows and columns and allow unlimited replay.

reading_support

Use one short instruction at a time, symbol narration, pictorial models and persistent labels for GROUPS, IN EACH and TOTAL.

attention_support

Follow a fixed BUILD-CHECK-COUNT-SAY routine with only the current group or tray active.

processing_support

Provide auto-place, stepwise distribution, undo, reduced choices and unlimited thinking time while preserving the mathematical target.

confidence_support

Do not score speed; preserve correct groups during repair and praise checking, grouping and inverse strategies.

eal_support

Preteach equal, group, each, altogether, row, share, divide, odd and even with gestures, objects and matched sentence frames.
