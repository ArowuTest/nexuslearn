Teaching Journey

warm_up

story-gate-open

Find where the sentence starts.

Activate the idea that a sentence has a clear beginning.

Audio: Look for the word that opens the sentence gate.

Visual: A story path begins at a glowing capital-letter gate.

concept_launch

sentence-path-draw

The sentence path needs a start gate and an end sign.

Introduce sentence punctuation as a boundary marker, not decoration.

Audio: A capital letter opens the sentence. A punctuation mark closes it.

Visual: Words travel along a path from capital gate to punctuation sign.

worked_example

question-bubble-pop

Where is my red hat? starts with a capital letter and ends with a question mark.

Model choosing punctuation from sentence meaning.

Audio: This sentence is asking something, so it needs a question mark.

Visual: A question bubble connects to the end mark.

guided_practice

word-tiles-snap

Drag the capital letter, words and end mark into the sentence path.

Practise sentence construction with explicit boundary cues.

Audio: Build one complete idea from start to end.

Visual: Word tiles snap into a path with fixed start and end slots.

checkpoint

end-mark-pulse

Is this telling, asking or shouting?

Diagnose whether the learner chooses punctuation by meaning.

Audio: Listen to what the sentence is doing.

Visual: Three end signs pulse: full stop, question mark, exclamation mark.

teach_back

companion-endmark-repair

Show Pip why this sentence needs a question mark.

Use explanation to confirm sentence-function understanding.

Audio: Can you teach me which end sign I need?

Visual: The companion offers a wrong end sign and waits for correction.
